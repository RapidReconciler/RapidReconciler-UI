Mailbox tour

rrsupport@getgsi.com is the single inbound address used across every GSI RapidReconciler document. As Helpdesk Tech, you read every inbound, identify what it is, and either resolve it or route it to the right tech — RR DBA, RR Network Tech, or RR DB Developer. The mailbox is a triage queue, not a ticketing system: read, classify, forward or resolve, note what you did.

Three principles

  • Read the subject first. The two structurally important emails — the pre-install submission and the database-ready notification — have predictable subject formats. Recognising those lets you route in seconds.
  • Route on signal, not on guess. If subject and body clearly identify the type, route confidently. If they don’t, treat the email as ambiguous — ask the sender to clarify.
  • Nothing falls through silently. Every inbound gets a routing decision (resolve, forward, redirect, or hold-and-clarify). The most common failure mode is an email sitting unread because no one was sure what to do with it.
Confirm with your manager

Response-time SLAs, ticketing-tool integration, shift handoff, and out-of-office coverage are team-specific and not covered here.

Customer support workflow

A meaningful share of inbound is generic customer support: a user reports something isn’t working, sends a screenshot of an error, asks why their data looks off. For these, paste the email or error text into the Help Desk scenario search; matching scenarios surface as you type, with step-by-step resolution inside each card.

Open the Help Desk
  1. Read the email for the gist. If the subject matches a structured type (see Inbound types), route via that table. Otherwise it’s a customer-support question — continue.
  2. Paste the email content into the Help Desk textarea. Subject + body + any error text. Results appear live.
  3. Pick the best-matching scenario and walk through it with the customer.
  4. Resolved: reply with what you found, note the resolution, done.
  5. Not resolved: forward to the right tech with what you tried — see When to escalate.

What this role covers

Beyond routing, the Helpdesk Tech is the first-line responder for inbound support. A meaningful portion of what arrives in rrsupport@getgsi.com doesn’t need a DBA or Network Tech — it needs someone who can read the question, gather a few details, and either resolve it or hand a clean ticket to the right person.

You can resolve

  • “How do I…” questions — point to the right KB page or walk through a documented procedure.
  • Information gathering — fill in missing details (customer, environment, error text, screenshots) before handing a ticket to a tech.
  • Login / access basics — redirect end-user account questions to the customer’s own RR administrator.
  • Known-issue lookups and status replies.

Out of scope — escalate

  • Database changes, schema work, SSIS edits → RR DBA.
  • VALC, agent rollout, certificate renewal → RR Network Tech.
  • Schema bugs or product-code issues → RR DB Developer.
  • Anything that touches a customer’s production environment.

Workflow

For every inbound that doesn’t fit a structured-routing pattern from the table above, the loop is:

  1. Acknowledge. One-line reply so the sender knows it landed. Silence is the worst outcome.
  2. Classify. Can you resolve, or does it need a tech?
  3. Gather. If details are missing (customer, environment, error text, screenshots), ask for everything you need in the first reply.
  4. Resolve. Apply the standard answer or walk the documented step; confirm the customer is unblocked.
  5. Escalate. If it’s beyond you, forward to the right tech with everything you gathered.
  6. Close the loop. Make sure the customer hears back when the tech resolves.
Set expectations in every reply

“I’ve forwarded this to our DBA team, you’ll hear back today” beats “we’re looking at it.” Customers don’t mind waiting if they know they’ve been heard.

When to escalate

Escalate when any of these are true:

  • The fix requires touching a database, VALC server, backend, or production environment.
  • You can’t reproduce the reported error against expected behaviour, and the KB doesn’t cover it.
  • You’ve tried the standard fix and it didn’t work.
  • The issue is customer-down or customer-degraded — escalate immediately, not at the end of your shift.
  • You’re unsure. A clean escalation that turns out to have been resolvable is cheap; a stalled attempt that should have escalated is expensive.

Where

  • Database, schema, SSIS, data load, rruser issues → RR DBA.
  • VALC, agent, web-interface access, certificate renewal → RR Network Tech.
  • Suspected product bug, schema change, “docs say it should work but doesn’t” → RR DB Developer.
  • Sales / contractual / renewal questions → redirect to their Sales contact.

How — JSM vs. email

  • JSM ticket (Jira Service Management, RapidReconciler Support, issue type Support) — the formal path. Use for anything that needs to be tracked, customer-down/degraded, or that you expect to take more than a same-day exchange. Include symptom, what you tried, customer + environment, and any artifacts.
  • Reply-all on the customer’s email thread — the informal path. Use when the conversation is already moving fast or the receiving tech has been looped in. Add the right tech to the thread; don’t restart the conversation elsewhere.

If an email thread needs tracking, the receiving tech opens the JSM ticket and replies with the link. Don’t double-create.

Escalate with context

The biggest favour you can do the receiving tech is hand them everything to start work: customer + environment, what the customer reported, what you tried and the outcome, error text/screenshots, timestamps, and the original email forwarded inline. A clean escalation saves a 20-minute back-and-forth.

What to do next

Three steps before your first shift:

  1. Read the two trigger-email examples — the Pre-Install Submission and the Database-Ready notification (Step 12) are both in Installing the Production Database. Seeing the actual structured content makes the routing patterns instant to recognise.
  2. Read the Common Requests reference on the VALC client-install page — the GSI-handles vs Customer-Admin-handles ownership matrix.
  3. Skim the Tech Team page — the three roles you route to most: RR DBA, RR Network Tech, RR DB Developer. (Your role lives on Help Desk; Tech Team is who you route to.)
If you have time before going solo

Shadow an experienced Helpdesk Tech for a handful of customer-support tickets. The “resolve vs escalate” judgment is hard to learn from a guide; easy to learn from watching someone make the call.
