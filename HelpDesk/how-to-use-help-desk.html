How-to guide

How to use the Help Desk.

Two surfaces, one goal — get you to a working answer fast. Here’s the path through each one.

The Help Desk page collects everything you might need when RapidReconciler isn’t behaving as expected. Two side-by-side surfaces handle the bulk of it: a search-driven runbook finder on the left, and an email-to-support form on the right.

Find your fix

Use this first

Paste an error message or describe what you’re seeing. Step-by-step runbooks for that exact symptom surface as you type.

Email RR Support

Fill in what you tried and what you saw. The button opens your mail client with the details pre-filled.

Part 1Find your fix — the search

Search by symptom, not by feature name

Drop whatever you have into the search box on the left card. The matcher works on the kind of language an RR user would actually use, not internal product jargon. Good examples:

  • “Status light is amber and hover shows the run has been going 3 hours”
  • “Cardex variance didn’t clear after I posted the JE”
  • “Inventory validation FAILED for company 00009”
  • “Page is blank after I log in to RapidReconciler”

You don’t need full sentences. A few keywords from the error or the symptom is usually enough. The matcher ignores common stopwords (the, is, what) and matches on the parts that carry meaning.

Paste long error blocks directly. Copilot-summarized emails, multi-line stack traces, screenshots’ OCR text — just paste the whole thing in. The matcher reads through it and surfaces runbooks for whatever symptoms it can identify. No need to clean it up first.

Keyboard shortcuts

Three shortcuts speed up the loop:

  • / from anywhere on the page focuses the search box.
  • Esc closes the currently open scenario and returns focus to the search box.
  • Ctrl+A inside the search box selects everything — useful when you want to paste a fresh symptom over the previous one.

Part 2Common scenarios — the shortcut tiles

Below the search box are three hand-picked tiles for issues that come up often:

  • Why is the System Status light red? — the Inventory Reconciliation page’s status indicator is showing red.
  • I can’t log in to RapidReconciler. — covers the agent-vs-server restart sequence and password-reset paths.
  • Page is blank after I log in. — browser cache, certificate, and proxy causes.

If one of these matches your symptom, click it directly — you’ll skip the search step.

Part 3Browse all documents — the drawer

Below the two main cards there’s a Browse all documents drawer. Open it when:

  • Your search returned nothing useful — sometimes the wording in your symptom doesn’t match the runbook’s title. Browsing the list of all available scenarios may surface a runbook that’s exactly right but tagged differently.
  • You want a concept doc, not a runbook — the drawer also lists RapidReconciler University documents: the “why” behind a feature, the JD Edwards table behind a number, a glossary lookup. These are reference reading rather than step-by-step fixes.
  • You’re browsing to learn — flip the drawer open and skim by topic. Each item shows its title plus a short category label so you can scan quickly.

What’s inside the drawer

Two top-level groups, each with module-specific sub-groups underneath:

  • Help Desk guides — this page’s how-to, plus the Log Analyzer for triaging server-side log files.
  • RapidReconciler University — the full customer documentation set, grouped by module (Getting Started, Inventory, A/P, Transfers, Administrators). Every doc on the University landing page is reachable from here too, so you don’t have to jump between pages.

Part 4Open a runbook — what to expect

Clicking a search result or browse-list item takes you to a dedicated runbook page. Every runbook follows the same shape:

  1. What you’re seeing. A short symptom recap so you can confirm you’re on the right page.
  2. Try this first. One or more numbered steps that resolve the issue at the customer site. Most runbooks open with the highest-success-rate fix.
  3. If it’s still broken — escalate. A pre-filled button that drafts an email to RR Support with the runbook’s name and the customer-side context already included.

When a runbook’s “Try this first” resolves the issue, you’re done — close the tab and get on with your day. When it doesn’t, use the escalation button at the bottom of the runbook page rather than starting a fresh email from scratch; it carries the runbook name and the steps you tried, which is the fastest way to a useful human reply.

Part 5Email RR Support — when no runbook fits

The right-hand card on the Help Desk page is for when the search and the drawer both come up empty. Fill in the two short fields:

  • What were you trying to do? One sentence — “Reconcile inventory for period 03 / 2026 before close”, “Investigate the cardex variance on item 545031”, etc.
  • Exact error message. Paste the literal text. Don’t paraphrase — the support team searches for it verbatim.

Click Email RR Support. Your default mail client opens with the support address, a subject line, and both fields stitched into the body. Add any attachments (screenshots, logs, exports) and send. Nothing flows to GSI through the browser — the email leaves only when you click Send in your own mail client.

Tip: include log output if you have it. The fastest replies come when the email already has the relevant log or error. If you have an agent log file or browser console output, run it through the Log Analyzer first, save the page or screenshot the result, and attach that. The classified output tells the responder exactly what was found, in seconds.

Part 6What to do if nothing’s working

A quick checklist when you’ve hit a dead end:

  1. Re-read what you typed. Sometimes the matcher is one keyword off from the right runbook. Try a different phrasing (the verb instead of the noun, the symptom instead of the error).
  2. Open the browse drawer and skim. A 30-second scan of the available scenarios will tell you whether the right runbook exists under a different title.
  3. Check the Log Analyzer. If your symptom involves the RapidReconciler agent or anything the application server logs, paste the relevant log into the Log Analyzer. It can tell you the verdict (OK / Review / Critical) and link you to the matching runbook.
  4. Email Support with what you have. When in doubt, send an email. A short message with what you tried beats a long delay while you keep searching. Support would rather get a half-formed question early than a polished one too late.
Found something that should be a runbook but isn’t? Mention it in your support email or send a note to rrsupport@getgsi.com. New runbooks are added regularly based on what real customer questions surface.
