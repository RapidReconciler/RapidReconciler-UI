Part 1 · Principles

Two principles to internalise

The full workflow chart and per-step detail live on the Client Management page — click any step in the chart to read what happens at that point and to open the related document. Before you do that, two things to keep in mind:

  • You don’t do the technical install. Your job ends when the customer emails RR Support to trigger the install. The RR DBA picks it up from there.
  • Only raise a PoC when necessary. The default path skips PoC entirely. PoCs are a real cost (billable GSI time on the customer’s servers) and should only be raised when a prospect needs to see RR running against their own data before signing.
Wrap-up

What to do next

You’ve read the principles. Three more suggested steps before your first prospect call:

  1. Read the Battlecard cover to cover. It’s the rep’s reference — pitch, ICP, pain triggers, discovery flow, and fast-answer FAQs. The talk track lives there.
  2. Skim the Provisioning Document and Installation Prep Document. You won’t fill these in — the customer does — but knowing what’s in them lets you answer the early objections (“how complicated is the install?”) without escalating to the tech team.
  3. Open the workflow chart on the Client Management page and click through every step. Each step’s sidebar pane explains what happens at that point and links the related document. Seeing the chart and reading the per-step detail at the same time is the fastest way to internalise the swim lanes.
