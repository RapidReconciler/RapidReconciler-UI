Documents

Process Documents

The five customer-facing and internal documents the sales workflow above references, listed in the order they come into play. Each one is linked from its corresponding flowchart step. The Proof of Concept document covers the optional PoC side path off Step 2 — only used when a PoC is necessary.

01 / A →

Proof of Concept

Used at Step 2A — the optional PoC side path. Covers when to propose a PoC, scope of the preliminary install (always run on the customer's servers), billable GSI time, and the preliminary agreement template. Sales should only raise the PoC option when necessary.
01 / B →

Provisioning Document

Sent by Sales at Step 3. Customer-facing technical requirements covering deployment topology, server specs, JD Edwards source platform connectivity, software & licensing breakdown, network requirements, and security posture. Customer reviews this with their I/T, security, and JDE teams before commercial conversations begin.
01 / C →

Contract Template

Generated and sent by Sales at Step 5, after a Yes on the Purchase Agreement decision. Standard contract that the customer signs to formalize the engagement before the discovery call is scheduled. Placeholder — document to be added.
01 / D →

Discovery Call

Used at Step 6. Agenda, participant list, and pre-call prep for the joint discovery call between Sales, Customer I/T, and RR DBA — the technical handoff that sets up the install.
01 / E →

Installation Prep Document

Sent to the customer at Step 7, after the discovery call. Walks Customer I/T through the pre-install steps they need to complete — topology selection, source platform identification, server provisioning, Windows security, and the pre-install checklist — and ends with the email handoff to rrsupport@getgsi.com that triggers the technical install.

Marketing

Collateral

Sales collateral for prospect outreach, demos, and follow-up. Use these alongside the process documents above to move a prospect from first conversation through to discovery call.

02 / A →

Self-Guided Tour

Interactive walk-through of RapidReconciler that prospects can run on their own time, without a live demo. Best used after first contact to qualify interest before booking a discovery call.
02 / B →

Overview

High-level product overview suitable for first-touch outreach and executive-level conversations. Covers what RapidReconciler does, who it’s for, and the key business outcomes.
02 / C →

Fact Sheet

One-page reference with feature list, supported environments, integration points, and licensing tiers. Best for technical evaluators and procurement.
