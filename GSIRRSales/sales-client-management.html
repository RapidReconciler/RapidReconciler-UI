Step 1

Identify Prospect

Sales

First step in the sales motion. Sales identifies a candidate organisation that may benefit from RapidReconciler — typically a JD Edwards customer with inventory reconciliation pain. The prospect doesn't know about RR yet at this point. No documents go out at this step; this is internal qualification.

Step 2

PoC needed? (decision)

Sales · decision point

Sales decides whether the deal needs a Proof of Concept before purchase. Most deals go straight to Step 3 (send provisioning); a PoC is only raised when the customer needs hands-on validation in their environment before committing. Yes branches into the optional PoC side path (Steps 2A → 2B). No proceeds to Step 3.

Step 2A

Send preliminary PoC agreement

Sales · PoC side path

Used at Step 2A — the optional PoC side path. Covers when to propose a PoC, scope of the preliminary install (always run on the customer's servers), billable GSI time, and the preliminary agreement template. Sales should only raise the PoC option when necessary.

Related documents
Proof of Concept → Statement of Work →
Step 2B

PoC engagement — preliminary install

Customer I/T · RR DBA · RR Network Tech

Three-way engagement: Customer I/T provides the environment, RR DBA installs the preliminary database, and RR Network Tech installs the agent. Always runs on the customer's own servers. No standalone document — the procedure and scope are covered inside the Proof of Concept document above. After the PoC concludes the deal returns to the main path at Step 3.

Step 3

Send provisioning document

Sales

Sent by Sales at Step 3. Customer-facing technical requirements covering deployment topology, server specs, JD Edwards source platform connectivity, software & licensing breakdown, network requirements, and security posture. Customer reviews this with their I/T, security, and JDE teams before commercial conversations begin.

View full document →
Step 4

Purchase Agreement? (decision)

Customer · decision point

Customer decides whether to proceed with purchase after reviewing the provisioning document. Yes moves to Step 5 (contract). No ends the engagement. This is the customer's decision, not Sales' — Sales' job at this point is to surface objections and clarify scope, not to push the close.

Step 5

Generate & send signed contract

Sales

Generated and sent by Sales at Step 5, after a Yes on the Purchase Agreement decision. Master Software License Agreement and Order Form. Sales fills in the customer-specific terms in the browser, prints to PDF, and uploads to DocuSign for signature. The signed contract triggers Step 6 (discovery call).

Related documents
Contract Template → Master Services Agreement →
Step 6

Arrange discovery call

Sales · RR DBA · RR Network Tech · Customer I/T

Used at Step 6. Agenda, participant list, and pre-call prep for the joint discovery call between Sales, Customer I/T, and the RR tech leads. The call surfaces anything unusual about the customer environment, walks them through what's coming next, and introduces the people on both sides before the install.

View full document →
Step 7

Send installation prep document

Sales

Sent to the customer at Step 7, after the discovery call. Walks Customer I/T through the pre-install steps they need to complete — topology selection, source platform identification, server provisioning, Windows security, and the pre-install checklist — and ends with the email handoff to rrsupport@getgsi.com that triggers the technical install.

View full document →
Step 8

Customer ready for install

Customer I/T → RR Support

Customer I/T finishes their pre-install work and emails rrsupport@getgsi.com with the install-prep submission. That email is the handoff out of the Sales workflow and into the Tech workflow — the technical install scheduling starts at this point. Sales' role on this deal is complete; tech-side handles the rest.
