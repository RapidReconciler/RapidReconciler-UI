Master Services Agreement

Document #:  ·  Effective Date:

THIS MASTER SERVICES AGREEMENT (this “MSA” or “Agreement”) shall be effective as of the date when both the Consultant and the Client have executed the same and delivered signatures hereunder (“Effective Date”). This Agreement is between Global Systems Integration, Inc. (“Consultant”), a Georgia corporation (“GSI”), and (“Client”), a corporation. This Agreement governs the provision of the services described in each Statement of Work and of Change Request entered into hereunder.

RECITALS

WHEREAS, Client desires Consultant to render professional services to Client’s company and Consultant will provide such professional services as it relates to technical support solutions.

WHEREAS, Consultant will consult with the Client and/or appointed Client Personnel concerning matters related to technical support solution products and services that relate to management and organization of the Client’s company.

WHEREAS, Based on the specific needs, services as described for Client in the Statement of Work, if any, which will be attached hereto and incorporated herein as an integral part hereof;

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual benefits, agreements, covenants and promises herein, and other good and valuable consideration, the sufficiency of which is hereby acknowledged, the parties hereto hereby agree as follows:

1.0   Definitions

In this Agreement:

“Change Request” (“CR”) means if Client desires to change, modify or supplement the services to be performed under a particular Statement of Work or if the Consultant believes a Statement of Work needs to be changed, modified or supplemented, the requesting party shall request such changes, modifications, or supplemental actions pursuant to a CR.

“Client Representative” A Client’s employees or agents designated by Client pursuant to Section 3(c), who shall be deemed as Client’s liaison with Consultant, and who shall have the power to act as Client’s project manager in order to make ongoing decisions under this Agreement which are binding upon Client.

“Consultant” Global Systems Integration, Inc. (“GSI”)

“Consultant Personnel” GSI employees, agents, or contractors.

“Deliverables” Any Developed Works, Consultant Works and Third-Party Works, or any combination thereof (including those identified as “Deliverables” in a Statement of Work, together with all CRs thereto), as well as any other items provided pursuant to the Services.

“MSA” the written Master Services Agreement between Consultant and Client, consist of terms and conditions which may include, appendices, addendums, exhibits, schedules, and other attachments hereto, including without limitation the Statement of Work and CR, if any, attached hereto.

“Personally Identifiable Information” Information that when used alone or with other relevant data can identify an individual.

“Preexisting Intellectual Property” Any and all (i) preexisting confidential information of GSI; (ii) preexisting reports, computer programs, documentation, specifications, hardware, products, work product, software, source code, algorithms, graphics, files, modifications, blueprints, diagrams, photographs, surveys, or other materials, writings, or works of authorship (and any drafts of the foregoing) created, developed, or prepared by GSI or licensed to GSI; (iii) tools or scripting applications developed or created by GSI or licensed to GSI during the performance of services under this Agreement; and (iv) patents, copyrights, trade secrets, trademarks and other Intellectual Property rights related (either directly or indirectly) to any of the foregoing.

“Services” The product and/or consulting services provided by Consultant in a Statement of Work hereunder, or as described in any Change Request.

“Statement of Work” (SOW) any and each document so named and from time to time prepared by Consultant and accepted by Client that will reflect the terms of engagement to provide Services to Client.

“Technical Support” Relating to Services provided by the Consultant to the Client through business, infrastructure, implementation, development, security, etc.

2.0   Terms and Conditions of Agreement

These terms and conditions will govern each party’s rights and obligations relating to GSI’s Services and/or GSI’s Consulting Services (collectively “Services”) hereunder. Consultant and Client intend that all Services provided by Consultant to Client hereunder be implemented through individual SOW or CR under this MSA (collectively “the Agreements”). Notwithstanding the foregoing, SOWs or CRs will be deemed to incorporate the terms and conditions of this MSA, unless the SOW or CR explicitly states otherwise. If there is any conflict between this MSA and a SOW/CR, the terms of the SOW/CR will control, but with respect to that particular SOW/CR.

3.0   Services: Statements of Work and Change Requests

3.1 Procedures Related to Statements of Work. Except as otherwise provided herein, Client shall authorize the performance of any services to be performed by Consultant by approving written SOWs from time to time (as amended, modified or supplemented by any applicable CRs). SOWs will be jointly prepared and executed by Consultant and Client for each project and shall not be binding upon the parties until so fully executed.

3.2 Change Request Procedures. Either party may propose changes to scope, nature or time schedule of the Services being performed under an applicable SOW. Requests for changes will be submitted to the other party in writing for consideration of feasibility and likely effect on the cost and schedule for performance of Services. The parties will mutually agree to any proposed changes, including resulting equitable adjustments to costs and schedules for the performance of Services. Proposed changes will be affected through written amendments to the affected SOW, signed by both parties (referred to as “CR”). Each CR shall correspond to the modified, amended or supplemented SOW.

3.3 Assistance to Consultant. Client agrees that it will cooperate with and assist Consultant and Consultant’s Personnel in the performance of the Services and will provide the resources necessary for Consultant’s performance hereunder as specified in the relevant SOW/CR or as otherwise required in the performance of the Services. Client or Client’s Representative shall be reasonably available to assist and cooperate with Consultant and Consultant’s Personnel for consultation, providing reasonable access to Client sites, providing other information and data required for the performance of the Services, and otherwise performing the responsibilities identified as being those of Client in the applicable Agreements.

4.0   Client’s Duties and Responsibilities

4.1 Data and Information. Client shall make available in a timely manner at no charge to Consultant all technical data, computer facilities, programs, files, documentation, test data, sample output, or other information and resources required by Consultant for the performance of the Services. Client will be responsible for, and assumes the risk of any problems resulting from, the content, accuracy, completeness and consistency of all such data, materials, and information supplied by Client.

4.2 Representative. Client or Client’s Representative will be the technical contact person for supporting the Client with respect to the Services provided by the Consultant. This contact person will support all Services tasked to the Consultant pursuant to this MSA, or any SOW/CR.

4.3 Equipment. During the time a Consultant is on Client’s or Client Representative’s premises, Client will provide at no charge to Consultant for such Consultant’s use an independent workplace and access to the appropriate computing resources and environment, including phone, fax, and other mutually agreed office equipment and materials needed for the work such Consultant is performing under this MSA.

4.4 Third-Party Licensing. Client shall ensure that all applicable licenses to authorize the Consultant to perform Services under a SOW are authorized licenses and in compliance with the obligations and assignment of licenses of the third-party Software Company.

4.5 Safe Work Environment. Client will be responsible for and will ensure that while Consultant and or Consultant Personnel are on Client premises, all proper and legal health and safety precautions are in place and fully operational to protect such persons, and Client will indemnify Consultant for any and all liability, direct damages, costs and expenses caused by the negligence or willful misconduct of Client/Client Representative employees, agents and/or subcontractors. Client will be responsible for and will ensure that while Consultant’s employees, agents or contractors are on Client’s premises, protections against hostile work environments are in place, additionally, all proper and legal health and safety precautions are in place and fully operational to protect such person.

5.0   Invoices and Payments

5.1 Compensation. Consultant shall render invoices for Services performed or to be performed in accordance with the terms under Section 5.2, and Client shall pay such invoices via Automated Clearing House (ACH) within fifteen (15) days of receipt, unless otherwise specified in the applicable SOW and/or CR. Currency of compensation shall be made in US dollars.

5.2 Invoices. Consultant will invoice Client in accordance with the payment schedule set forth in the relevant SOW/CR. If any payment is not made when due, Consultant will be entitled to suspend performance of the Services immediately and, at its option, to terminate the relevant SOW/CR in accordance with its terms.

5.2(i) If Client disputes any invoice, Client shall timely pay the undisputed portion and promptly notify Consultant in writing, within five (5) business days of the nature of the dispute regarding the remainder. Any such dispute must be for a good faith reason. The parties will use their best efforts to negotiate in good faith to resolve any disputes expeditiously.

5.2(ii) Client shall not withhold any undisputed amounts due hereunder and Consultant reserves the right to cease work without prejudice if undisputed amounts are not paid when due.

The invoices submitted to Client shall indicate the following:

a. Description of Services performed during the period;
b. Detailed charges for Consultant;
c. Summary of Reimbursable Expenses (as defined below).

5.3 Payment. If any amount owing by Client under this MSA or SOW/Change Request (collectively, “the Agreements”) for Consultant Services is more than 30 days overdue, without limiting Consultant’s rights or remedies:

5.3(i) Charges will accrue late interest at the rate of 1.5% of the outstanding balance per month, or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower, and/or

5.3(ii) Consultant will be entitled to suspend its performance of the Services immediately and will have the option to terminate the relevant Agreement immediately without such termination being deemed a breach or subjecting Consultant to any penalty or fees.

5.4 Expenses. Client shall reimburse Consultant for reasonable out-of-pocket expenses, including travel, lodging, and meals (“Reimbursable Expenses”), as set forth in a SOW and for any non-standard expenses incurred at the written request of Client. Clients and Consultants will discuss traveling arrangements within a timely fashion to ensure reasonable costs. Consultant will abide by Consultant’s travel policy, available upon request. In the event Client requests itemized expenses with individual receipts, an administration fee based on a scale of $75 per will be added to the invoice.

5.5 Taxes. Amounts due under this Agreement are payable to Consultant without deduction for any tax, tariff, duty, or assessment imposed by any government authority (national, state, provincial, or local), including without limitation any sales, use, excise, ad valorem, property, withholding, or value-added tax, whether or not withheld at the source (collectively, “Sales Tax”). Except as forbidden by applicable law, Consultant may require that Client submit applicable Sales Taxes to Consultant. However, the preceding sentence does not apply to the extent that Client is tax exempt, provided it gives Consultant a valid tax exemption certificate within 30 days of the Effective Date. Consultant’s failure to include any applicable tax in an invoice will not waive or dismiss its rights or obligations pursuant to this Section 5.5. If applicable law requires withholding or deduction of Sales Taxes or any other tax or duty, Client shall separately pay Consultant the withheld or deducted amount, over and above fees due. For the avoidance of doubt, this Section 5.5 does not govern taxes based on Consultant’s net income.

5.6 International Travel. To the extent applicable, clients who are physically based outside North America will be billed at 50% of their standard international billing rate for international travel time. International Clients shall provide business class travel arrangements for Consultants whose flights are in the excess of eight (8) hours.

6.0   Independent Contractor

Consultant, in all matters relating to this MSA, shall act as an independent contractor. Except to the extent expressly authorized by this MSA, neither party hereto is an agent, legal representative, partner, joint venture, participant in a joint enterprise, franchisee, employee, or servant of the other party hereto for any purpose. Neither party hereto will represent that it has any authority to assume or create any obligation, express or implied, on behalf of the other party hereto, or to represent the other party hereto in any capacity. Unless otherwise noted each party shall be responsible for all expenses incurred by it under this MSA.

7.0   Termination

7.1 Term of Agreement. This MSA shall remain in full force and effect for as long as the pertinent SOW is active or for a term of four (4) years. This Agreement will automatically be renewed for successive one-year periods, unless a party provides the other with written notice at least sixty (60) calendar days prior to its next scheduled expiration stating that it does not wish for this Agreement to be renewed. If this MSA expires, its terms and conditions will continue to apply to any SOW then in effect until the SOW expires or terminates.

7.2 Terms of Termination.

7.2(i) Client may terminate the MSA anytime without cause upon thirty (30) days’ prior written notice to Consultant; provided there are no active SOWs for a given engagement. Upon such termination, or upon expiration of this Agreement, Client shall immediately pay all open invoices as set forth in a SOW. In no event will termination relieve Client of Client’s obligation to pay any fees payable to Consultant for the period prior to the effective date of termination.

7.2(ii) Consultant may terminate this MSA: (a) without cause, upon thirty (30) days’ written notice to Client; or (b) immediately and without further notice if a dispute over an unpaid invoice is not resolved within fifteen (15) days after receipt of notification by Consultant, as more fully set forth in section 5.2 of this MSA. Reasonably promptly after either party’s notice of termination, Consultant shall assist Client with transition to an alternate vendor or to in-house operations pursuant to Consultant’s then-standard transition policies; provided (a) Consultant has no good faith basis to believe that Client is in breach of this Agreement and (b) Client pre-pays Consultant’s standard fees for such services. Consultant has no obligation to provide such assistance or to retain any copies of Client data more than one hundred eighty (180) days after termination of this Agreement.

7.2(iii) Either party may terminate this MSA at any time, with the exception of nonpayment of undisputed amounts, if (a) the other party materially breaches any of its obligations hereunder or under the Agreements and such breach has not been cured within thirty (30) days of written notice specifying the nature of the breach; (b) either party attempts to assign this Agreement or any obligation hereunder without the other party’s consent, except as otherwise allowed hereunder; or (c) any assignment is made of the other party’s business for the benefit of creditors, or if a petition in bankruptcy is filed by or against the other party, or if a receiver or similar officer is appointed to take charge of all or part of the other party’s property, or if the other party is adjudicated in a bankruptcy proceeding.

7.3 Consequences of Termination. Upon the expiration or termination of the Agreements, Consultant shall return to Client all materials and items furnished to Consultant where materials and items have been developed by Consultant in the performance of Services under this Agreement subject to Client’s full payment of amounts due to Consultant.
