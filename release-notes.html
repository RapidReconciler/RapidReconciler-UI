What’s new in RapidReconciler.

Newest changes appear at the top — each entry is auto-published from a merge to main. Spot something missing or want context on a fix? rrsupport@getgsi.com.

May 13, 2026 ff59c5a

Broaden Step 3’s third decision-table entry on the inventory-validation-light-red scenario to accept both GLOK = No and GLOK = End. The Account Repost (R099102) path applies in both cases when UnpostBatch is zero on every row — limiting the entry to “GLOK = No” alone was missing the GLOK = End variant customers hit in the field.

May 13, 2026 f2bdbb6

Teach the Transaction Detail analyzer to recognize when a return / credit doc has both stock and non-stock lines under the same order type — a customer-confirmed pattern where JDE DMAAIs (which route by order type + GL class only, not line type) push the dollars-only credit lines through the inventory AAI just like the stock returns. The cardex captures the real inventory movement correctly; the GL takes the misrouted credit half and ends up wrong.

Field example: doc 1071546 RI, where the analyzer was producing pattern 5.3 “Cardex-Only — go post the unposted batch”, which would have pushed the misrouted credits into GL and made the close-time cleanup worse.

May 13, 2026 b59cab7

Fix the cross-card vertical alignment on the cover destinations by taking the privacy badge out of the card’s flow. It was sitting between the title and the description on the Export Analyzer card and pushing that card’s description ~30px lower than the matching elements on the University and Help Desk cards.

May 13, 2026 7987626

Rename the third destination from “Analysis Workbook Generator” to “RapidReconciler Export Analyzer” across all customer-visible touch points, and add a privacy badge to its card on the cover.

May 12, 2026 c63d85c

Teach the Transaction Detail analyzer to recognize a missing variance/revaluation AAI (typically DMAAI 4141) when a Standard Cost Change row in F4111 has no GL counterpart. Field example: doc 545031 IC had a +$3,191.93 completion that posted to F4111 and F0911 cleanly, plus a -$287.91 “Standard Cost Change” row in F4111 with NO matching F0911 entry on either the inventory or expense side. The variance equalled the orphan amount exactly. The analyzer was producing Pattern 5.4 “Account or Period Mismatch” with a generic “check DMAAI configuration” hint — accurate-ish but useless for actually fixing the customer’s issue.

May 12, 2026 4c0433f

Capture a real customer remediation in the “I can’t log in” scenario: when the rr-valc-agent service is already Running but logins still fail with “server unavailable”, the backend is wedged. Restart the service first; if that doesn’t clear it, reboot the RR app server. A recent customer email confirmed a full server reboot resolved exactly this symptom, which the existing IT-email checklist didn’t suggest.
