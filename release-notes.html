May 15, 2026

RapidReconciler Help Desk and Export Analyzer ship a few customer-facing improvements:

- "Browse all documents" drawer on the Help Desk now lists every document in the RR University set (35 docs, organized by module) alongside the Help Desk's own guides. You can browse the whole knowledge base without leaving the Help Desk. - New "How to use the Help Desk" guide walks through the page: search by symptom, the common-scenario shortcuts, the browse drawer, opening a runbook, the email-support escape hatch, and what to do if nothing's working. - The Email RR Support form is now shorter — just two fields ("What were you trying to do" and "Exact error message"). The "When did it happen" field was removed; timestamp isn't useful for routing your support email. - All the form placeholder examples are now RR-context wording (System Status light, cardex variance, inventory validation, period reconciliation) instead of the install/server-side errors you'd never actually see as an end user. - Export Analyzer's Transaction Detail Pattern 5.6 ("Standard Cost Change after work order completion") gets smarter when you've loaded your DMAAI integrity report on Step 1: instead of "credit AAI 3260 or AAI 3240," the diagnosis names the actual GL account configured for your company and GL class. If neither AAI is configured, the diagnosis flips to "configure the AAI first" instead of chasing the R30837 sequencing path.

May 15, 2026

The RapidReconciler Export Analyzer now ships several customer-facing improvements:

- Step 1 DMAAI loader. Drop your DMAAI integrity report (Integrity Report 0 from RapidReconciler Inventory Integrity page) once per browser session. A small status pill at the top of Step 1 turns green when loaded; the analyzer can then surface the actual GL accounts your AAIs resolve to in upcoming Transaction Detail diagnoses. Loading is optional — most exports do not need it.

- "About these analyses" disclaimer on the analyzer landing page. A collapsible band sets expectations honestly: the analyzer is a pattern matcher, customer configurations vary, and diagnoses should be verified in JDE before posting journal entries or changing configuration. Includes a one-click feedback link if a finding does not fit.

- "Verify before acting" block on every output Analysis tab. A persistent reminder right at the top of the analysis that the finding is a pattern-match, not a verdict.

- New Notes tab on every output workbook. Structured prompts let your team capture whether the diagnosis was right and, if not, what the real cause was — plus a one-click email link to send the workbook back to RR support for pattern-library expansion. Nothing leaves your machine except via your own email client — your workbook stays on your laptop.

- Landing-page cleanup. Removed a redundant navy logo glyph from the Export Analyzer, RR University, and Help Desk welcome banners. The remaining wordmark and GSI logo identify each surface cleanly.

Previously, returning visitors whose browser had cached the search index would not see newly added documents in search results until they hard-refreshed the page. May 15, 2026

Significant new content in RapidReconciler University's Inventory module, all sourced from the JD Edwards Inventory Management Implementation Guide:

• Inventory reconciliation — added a section explaining JDE's own R41500 (batch) and P41500 (interactive) Inventory-to-G/L reconciliation programs and how RR fits alongside them (RR investigates and resolves variances; the JDE programs stamp matched documents as reconciled in F4111 and F0911 after the fact). Added a "batches to confirm with your DBA before reconciling" checklist covering Sales Update, Manufacturing Accounting, Voucher Match / Post, and G/L Post.

• Cycle counts and tag counts — documented what the variance journal from R41413 and R41610 actually contains, and added a warning about the JDE rule that a count entered at a new item-location writes a variance for the entire counted quantity (so a routine count can look like a large shrinkage).

• Item ledger — added a subsection on R41548 (As-Of Updating) explaining why skipping it produces false-positive variances on low-velocity items even when F4111 is correct.

• Item ledger — added a new section on Item Reclassifications (P4116) explaining how paired From / To entries can credit one inventory account and debit a different one, with no purchase or sales activity behind them — the "phantom GL-class movement" pattern in the cardex.

• Inventory costing — added the JDE rule that weighted average and actual cost cannot be used as the sales / inventory cost method for items with stocking type C or R, plus guidance on diagnosing unit-cost movements that show up in the cardex even when on-hand quantity looks fine.

May 15, 2026

When opening a JDE-vs-RR reconciliation question with RR Support, the "Comparing RR to JDE Reports" doc now asks for a per-item example with the JDE F4111 cardex export and the RR As Of Item Details export for the same item — those two transaction lists side-by-side are how we identify which variance source is responsible. If multiple variance patterns are observed, send one example per pattern. The email template in the doc has been updated to match.

May 15, 2026

The "Comparing RR to JDE Reports" doc has a new concept page on Cardex variance — what it is, why F4111 and F41021 can drift apart, how to identify it on the Inventory Reconciliation page, and how to tell it apart from a cardex date-logic issue. The doc is now self-contained so it can be emailed as a single URL to a customer reviewing a reconciliation question.

May 15, 2026

The "Comparing RR to JDE Reports" doc picks up two more sections. The rounding-residual explanation now describes JDE's own Zero Balance Adjustment mechanism — what it clears automatically and which transaction types it doesn't fire on, which is where most of the residual lines RR shows actually come from. The Cardex date logic page now includes a step-by-step "How to verify with the As Of Item Details export" workflow for diagnosing a quantity-on-hand mismatch against JDE.

May 15, 2026

The "Comparing RR to JDE Reports" doc gets two additions. The Stock Valuation topic now explains how the rounding differs between JDE's report (quantity x unit cost rounded to 2 decimals at display time) and RR's summed cardex amounts, and why customers see zero-quantity lines with small residuals on the As-of view — and why those lines aren't suppressed. A new "Cardex date logic" concept page documents the four-rule period assignment (GL Date or Creation Date, with future dates pulled back to today) and the Inventory Basis vs. GL Basis accounting methods so customers can predict which period a cardex row will land in.
