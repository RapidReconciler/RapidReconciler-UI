Start Here — A/P Clerks

A wayfinding guide for accounts payable clerks new to RapidReconciler. JD Edwards experience is assumed throughout. The focus here is the PO Receipts module — the Received-Not-Vouchered (RNV) reconciliation, suspensions and exclusions, and the AP-side journal entries that clear what voucher matching alone cannot.

i

Module availability. If you can’t find the PO Receipts module in your main navigation, contact your RapidReconciler administrator. They will either grant you access (if the module is already enabled at your site) or open a request with GSI support to enable it.

Who This Is For

You are responsible for some part of the AP cycle that interacts with PO Receipts: investigating open RNV balances, deciding when to suspend or exclude an order, posting offsetting journal entries to clear unvouchered residuals, and supporting period-end close on the RNV account.

This guide assumes you already understand JDE’s P4314 voucher match process, what F43121 is, and how Match Type 1 (receipts) and Match Type 2 (vouchers) records combine to drive the RNV account. What it does is map that JDE knowledge to RapidReconciler’s vocabulary — particularly the Suspension and Exclusion mechanisms, which exist only in RapidReconciler — and tell you what to read, in what order, in your first month.

What “Ready” Looks Like

Use these milestones to gauge your own progress. They are not prescriptive timelines — some sites move faster, some take longer — but they are the typical learning arc for a JDE-experienced AP user.

Day 1

Logged in. Can find the PO Receipts module, the unreconciled view, and the RNV variance summary. Can identify your accounts in the filter.

Week 1

Can drill from the variance summary into a specific order, read the F43121 vs GL match status, and explain when an order should be suspended, excluded, or left alone.

Month 1

Has applied at least one suspension and one exclusion with a defensible audit-trail note. Has posted an offsetting JE in JDE that cleared a known-unresolvable RNV balance.

What RapidReconciler Does (and Doesn’t)

Five minutes of orientation. The aim is to translate what you already know about JDE into where it lives and what it’s called inside RapidReconciler — and to set boundaries on what RapidReconciler can and cannot change.

The Read-Only Model

RapidReconciler does not write to JDE. It reads from JDE on a nightly import cycle, compares the data, and surfaces discrepancies. Every correction — every voucher match, every JE, every PO cancellation — is posted in JD Edwards using the tools you already know. RapidReconciler then picks up the change at the next nightly refresh.

RapidReconciler can changeOnly JD Edwards can change
Notes attached to a PO order or line; suspensions (whole order) and exclusions (single line); filter and view preferences; report exports. F43121 (the PO Receiver table), the GL, every Match Type 1 receipt and Match Type 2 voucher record — everything that matters for the RNV reconciliation.

The practical implication: if you spot an unresolvable RNV residual at 2pm and post a clearing JE in JDE at 3pm, the F43121 record will still show as open in RapidReconciler until tomorrow’s import. The suspension or exclusion mechanism (which is in RapidReconciler) is what removes the item from the variance calculation in the meantime.

What It’s Comparing

The headline question RapidReconciler answers for PO Receipts is one you already know how to ask: does the open balance in F43121 agree to the GL balance for the RNV account?

In a clean environment, every Match Type 1 (receipt) record is eventually offset by a Match Type 2 (voucher) record, the F43121 open balance reduces to what is genuinely awaiting voucher, and the GL agrees. In practice, certain records will never clear through the standard process — receipts cleared by manual JE, accrual-only landed costs, out-of-sequence reversals, cancelled POs with rounding residuals. Those are the items the suspension and exclusion mechanisms exist to handle.

If you have RR access, log in and navigate to the PO Receipts module. Open any open PO order and try to change something on it — edit a quantity, change a date, alter a price.

You can’t. The fields are read-only. That’s the read-only model in action: every correction you’ll ever make to PO data lands in JD Edwards using the tools you already know. RapidReconciler shows you what needs attention, but doesn’t change anything.

Your First Session

A 10-minute guided first sit-down at the PO Receipts module. The aim is not to do real work — it’s to confirm you can find each landmark and read what it says. If the RNV variance is non-zero at the end, that’s expected. Investigation comes later.

Before You Start

You need a RapidReconciler login (provided by your administrator) and a browser. JDE access is not required for this session — you’re only looking, not posting.

The 10-Minute Walk

#ActionWhat To Look For
1 Log in. You land on the default landing page. Navigate to the PO Receipts module from the top navigation. Bookmark this URL for later.
2 Find the status indicators. Confirm that the latest import has succeeded before reviewing data. If anything is red or yellow, hover for a tooltip — do not act on the data until the indicators are green.
3 Open the period selector. Pick the most recently closed period (not the current open one). Closed periods are stable; the current period changes daily as receipts are posted in JDE.
4 Apply your account filter. Filter to the RNV account(s) you’re responsible for. The filter applies left-to-right; removing a company also removes its child entities.
5 Read the RNV variance summary. The headline numbers: the GL balance for the RNV account, the open F43121 total, and the variance between them. Zero variance means F43121 agrees to the GL.
6 Open the unreconciled view. Lists every open Match Type 1 record contributing to the F43121 open total. Don’t investigate yet — just see the shape of what’s open.
7 Drill into one order. Click into any open order. You’ll see the order header, the lines, and the Edit Note button (the control for suspensions and exclusions). Don’t click Edit Note yet — but locate it.
8 Log out, take a break. You’ve seen the page. The next session is when you start asking why a specific F43121 record is open and whether it will ever clear.
i

If something doesn’t look right — for example, the RNV GL balance differs from JDE by a large amount, or the period selector is empty — stop and ask your administrator. These usually indicate a configuration or permission issue, not a real reconciliation problem.

Try this now

The 8-step walk above is meant to be done, not just read. If you have RR access, work through the eight steps in your own application before continuing to the next topic.

Don’t aim for understanding everything you see — aim for being able to find each landmark. If you got stuck on any step (especially finding the Edit Note button on a specific order), that’s the part to ask your reviewer or administrator about.

Try this now

If your environment has any suspended orders or excluded lines, find one and read its audit-trail note. Does the note explain what was done, why it’s acceptable to remove from variance, and reference a JE or ticket number?

If yes, that’s a good model for the notes you’ll write. If no — if the note is something like “Cleared by JE” with no further detail — that’s a real example of what not to do, and it’s worth knowing your team has produced these in the past.

If your environment has no suspensions or exclusions yet (typical for new deployments), browse a few open F43121 records in the unreconciled view to get a feel for what the items look like before you start making decisions about them.

Stop and Ask If…

Three signals that mean stop reading and find a colleague or your administrator. None of these are failures — they’re the questions experienced users still ask each other.

i

The RNV GL balance in RapidReconciler doesn’t match what JDE shows. The numbers come from JDE. Disagreement means your filter, your period, or your account selection is off. Don’t investigate variances until the GL number agrees with JDE.

i

You’re about to suspend an order but you’re not certain the GL is correct. Suspensions and exclusions remove items from the variance calculation; they don’t fix anything. If the GL is wrong, the JE has to come first. Ask your reviewer before suspending in this situation.

i

You’re uncertain whether something is a suspension or an exclusion. The decision guide answers this in four questions, but the judgment behind “does this affect the whole order or just one line?” can be ambiguous. When in doubt, prefer an exclusion — it’s the more precise tool and it leaves more activity visible in the standard view.

Your First Period-End Close

The transition from monitoring RNV variances to closing and posting. The procedure is fully documented in the PO Receipts Reconciliation Walkthrough. This topic covers the things around the procedure: how most sites approach a first close, and the three mistakes that bite new AP users hardest.

Two Recommendations Before You Close Live

Neither of these is enforced by the application. Both are worth the time.

Pair With an Experienced Reviewer for the First Close

Have someone who has closed before sit with you (or be on a call) for your first end-to-end period close. Their job is not to do the work — it is to catch the small judgment calls that the documentation can’t capture (in particular, suspension-vs-exclusion decisions on real orders, and the materiality threshold your team applies). Most failures on a first close are not procedural mistakes; they are confidence mistakes.

Walk a Closed Prior Period Before Closing Live

Before your first live close, walk through a closed prior period in the application without changing anything. Look at the orders that were suspended or excluded in that close, read the audit-trail notes, and confirm you understand why each decision was made. The reasoning behind suspension and exclusion choices is the part of the job that doesn’t come from JDE experience — seeing how your team has handled it before is the fastest way to internalize the local standard.

If a non-production environment is available, even better: re-run the close in non-production, applying suspensions and exclusions yourself, and compare your decisions to what was actually done.

Pre-Close Checklist

Before you post the closing journal entry, confirm the following:

  • Pre-close requirements
  • The latest import has succeeded; status indicators are green.
  • All open F43121 records that should clear through standard voucher matching have done so — AP has worked through the in-flight match queue.
  • Each remaining unresolvable F43121 record has been reviewed and either suspended or excluded with a defensible audit-trail note.
  • The aggregate suspension and exclusion totals tie to the GL clearing JE you’re about to post.
  • Any prior-period suspensions intended as temporary (cross-period timing) have been reviewed for reversal.

Top Three New-User Mistakes

Drawn from the Best Practices and Pitfalls sections of the existing PO Receipts docs, narrowed to the three that specifically catch first-time closers.

!

1. Suspending an order to hide a real problem. A suspension is an annotation, not a correction. If the GL is wrong, fix the GL first — suspending the order won’t fix the underlying issue and will make next month’s reconciliation harder. The test: if you can’t write a defensible note explaining why removing this item from the variance is appropriate, you’re probably suspending too soon.

!

2. Vague audit-trail notes. “Cleared by JE” or “Per accounting” will not survive an audit review six months later. Every note must say what the issue is, why removing the item is acceptable, the JE or ticket reference, and the date. The example notes in Suspensions & Exclusions → Audit Trail Note show what good looks like.

!

3. Not reviewing prior suspensions for new activity. If you suspend a whole order whose OT is still open in JDE, future receipts can land against it — and because the order is suspended, those receipts will not appear in the variance calculation. Each period-end close should include a check of previously-suspended orders for new activity. If new activity is found, unexclude/re-exclude is the standard correction.

If Your First Close Has a Non-Zero Variance After Posting

It happens. The Audit Report tells you what was suspended, what was excluded, and what JEs were posted. Review it with your buddy. Most causes fall into one of these buckets:

  • An F43121 record contributed to the variance that should have been suspended or excluded but wasn’t.
  • A suspension or exclusion was applied but the matching JE was not posted (or vice versa).
  • The post-JE nightly refresh hadn’t completed when the variance was checked — check again the next morning.
  • New voucher activity posted in JDE between the close and the import.

None of these is a failure of the close itself. They’re learning events. Document what happened in your team’s notes — the second close benefits enormously from the first one’s post-mortem.

Self-Check

Six scenarios to test your reading. Read each one, decide what you’d do, and click Where to find this to confirm. If a scenario surprises you or you’d guess wrong, that’s the topic to re-read — not a failure. The questions are deliberately scenario-based rather than fact-based; the suspension/exclusion decisions in this job reward judgment more than rule recall.

Scenario 1

A purchase order has 5 lines. Lines 1, 2, and 3 vouchered cleanly through P4314. Line 4 was cleared by a manual JE because the supplier billed it on a separate invoice. Line 5 has an orphaned reversal that IT confirms cannot be re-matched.

Suspend the whole order, exclude lines 4 and 5 individually, or some combination? Why?

Is it acceptable to suspend now and verify the GL later? What does the doc recommend, and why?

Scenario 4

Three months ago, an admin suspended an entire PO because all its receipts had been cleared by JE. This month, you notice the order is still suspended — but JDE shows new receipt activity has posted against the same OT.

Is the new activity contributing to the variance calculation? What do you do?

You see an open F43121 record from a receipt dated 12/29 against an order that was vouchered 1/3 in the next period. The auditor has flagged it as a variance for the December close.

Vocabulary

RapidReconciler-specific terms that aren’t standard JDE, with focus on the PO Receipts module. Each entry is a one-line definition plus a link to the doc that explains it in depth. Skim once for awareness; come back when a term shows up on screen and you want to remember what it meant.

Audit-trail note
The note entered through the Edit Note popup that documents why a suspension or exclusion was applied. Required — an annotation without context cannot be defended in audit. Suspensions & Exclusions → The Audit Trail Note.
Edit Note button
The single point of control for both applying and reversing suspensions and exclusions. The same button handles both actions; the checkbox in the popup is the toggle.
Exclusion
A line-level annotation that removes a single PO line from the variance calculation while leaving the rest of the order visible. Used when the issue is contained to one line on an otherwise active order. Suspensions vs. Exclusions.
Exclusion (In Transit module)
Different from PO Receipts exclusion. In the In Transit module, an exclusion applies at the order-pair level (ST/OT) and clears unbalanced transfer order pairs. Same word, different mechanism. Transfer Order Exclusion Guide.
F43121
The JD Edwards PO Receiver table — the source of truth for open receipts. RapidReconciler reads from it on every nightly import and never modifies it. Standard JDE term, included here because it’s the central object of the RNV reconciliation.
Match Type 1 / Match Type 2
Match Type 1 = receipt record. Match Type 2 = voucher record. In a clean reconciliation, every Match Type 1 is offset by a Match Type 2; orphaned Match Type 1 records are the typical candidates for suspension or exclusion.
RNV (Received-Not-Vouchered)
The clearing GL account that holds value for goods received but not yet vouchered. The reconciliation goal is to make this account balance to F43121’s open total.
Suspension
An order-level annotation that removes an entire PO from the variance calculation. Used when the whole order is non-actionable — for example, when a JE has cleared the entire RNV residual and the F43121 records are orphaned. Suspensions vs. Exclusions.
Unexclude / Re-exclude procedure
The way to handle a previously-excluded line that has had new activity. Remove the exclusion, review the new activity, then re-apply the exclusion. Applying & Reversing.
Unreconciled view
The default view in the PO Receipts module — lists every open Match Type 1 record contributing to the F43121 open total. Suspended orders and excluded lines are removed from this view (and have their own filtered views).
Variance Calculation section
The breakdown of the RNV variance into attributable sources, including a distinct line for the aggregate of suspensions and exclusions. The number on that line is the value of the offsetting JE you’ll post in JDE.
