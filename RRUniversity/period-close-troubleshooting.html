Period Close Troubleshooting

Period-end is the highest-stakes window in the reconciliation cycle. When something doesn’t complete the way it should, you need a fast answer. Pick the symptom that matches what you’re seeing — most cases resolve before you need to email support.

i

RapidReconciler is read-only by design. It diagnoses variances and surfaces them on the dashboard; corrections happen in JDE. If the close won’t complete or a balance won’t clear, the fix almost always lives upstream in JDE (post a batch, correct a transaction, post a balancing JE) — the topics below tell you where in JDE to act.

Period won’t close

The period won’t close cleanly, the close action is greyed out, you can’t advance to the new period, or the new period isn’t available in the period selector dropdown. RapidReconciler treats period close as gated on data being clean — which means the blocker is almost always something upstream that needs to be addressed before close can complete.

The most common cause: unposted batches in JDE

The reconciliation engine relies on the prior period’s GL batches being fully posted in JDE before it can roll forward. If any GL batch for the closing period is still unposted, the period close will fail or be blocked.

  1. In JDE, look for any unposted GL batches in the period you’re trying to close.
  2. Post them.
  3. Wait for the next RapidReconciler refresh (or trigger one manually if your role allows), then return and try the close again.

If batches are all posted but close still fails

  • Check the Inventory Validation indicator at the top of the Reconciliation page. If it’s red, the roll-forward has a variance — resolve that first. See Validation light is red.
  • Check the System Status indicator. If that’s red, a back-end job has crashed and close won’t complete until the job recovers. See the system-status-red scenario.
  • Confirm you’re looking at the right period. The Period End Selector at the top right of the module status bar drives what you see — make sure it’s set to the period you’re closing.
  • Confirm your permissions. Some close actions are restricted to specific roles. If you used to be able to close periods and can’t now, your RR Administrator may have changed your permissions.

If the new period isn’t in the dropdown

If you can close the prior period but the next period isn’t showing up as a selectable option, this usually points at a configuration or data issue your administrator or RR support needs to look at — the period-end roll didn’t complete cleanly.

Email rrsupport@getgsi.com with the company, account, and the period you expected to see.

!

If batches are posted, status lights are green, and the close still won’t complete, see Email RR Support.

Validation light is red

The Inventory Validation indicator at the top of the Reconciliation page has turned red, often paired with an “out of balance” or variance message. The carry-forward calculation panel typically shows an unreconciled amount.

What this means

The roll-forward from the prior period failed to balance. RapidReconciler is telling you that the closing position in the prior period and the opening position in the current period don’t reconcile — usually because something happened between them that the system can’t account for.

The first thing to check (resolves ~80% of cases)

Hover your cursor over the red indicator. The product’s own tooltip will tell you the most common cause: unposted GL batches in the prior period.

  1. In JDE, check whether any GL batches for the prior period are still unposted.
  2. If you find unposted batches, post them.
  3. Refresh the Reconciliation page in RapidReconciler.
  4. The indicator should go green — the carry-forward will recompute against the now-posted data.

If all prior-period batches are confirmed posted

Click the red indicator to export the variance Excel report. This is the artifact RR support needs to investigate transaction-level activity. See the inventory-validation-red scenario for the full runbook including what to attach.

If the red light is paired with other symptoms

If you’re also seeing a red System Status light, see the System status light is red scenario — that runbook covers the IT-side environment checks (disk, TempDB, SQL Agent, SSIS) that often clear paired symptoms.

!

If all batches are posted and the variance Excel doesn’t reveal an obvious culprit, see Email RR Support with the Excel attached.

Audit report won’t generate

The audit report is your sign-off artifact for period close. If it won’t generate, exports empty, or errors out, you can’t close cleanly until it produces a valid output.

Try these steps in order

  1. Check the System Status light first. If it’s red, the back-end job has crashed and the audit report can’t complete until the job recovers. See the system-status-red scenario.
  2. Confirm the period selector at the top right is set to the period you’re trying to audit, not the new period or a different one.
  3. Hard-reload the page with Shift+F5 and try again — clears any cached state from a previous run that might be holding the audit in a broken intermediate state.
  4. If the audit downloads but is empty, apply the same checks as a regular export: filters, period selector, permission scope. See the “Export is empty / wrong” topic in Reports & Exports Troubleshooting.
  5. If the audit downloads but figures look wrong, check the Inventory Validation indicator. A red indicator means the period had a variance — the audit is reflecting that, not generating a new error. Resolve the validation issue first (see Validation light is red).
  6. Try a smaller scope. Run the audit against a single business unit or company to see if the issue is data-volume or report-wide.

If the audit consistently won’t complete

If the audit is reproducibly failing for everyone at your site and status lights are green, the problem is likely on the GSI side — the audit job has stalled, or the underlying SQL Agent job that prepares audit data has stopped. See the stale-data scenario.

!

If steps above don’t resolve it, see Email RR Support with the report name, the period, the filters, and a screenshot of the failure.

Carry-forward looks wrong on day 1 of the new period

You opened the new period and the opening balances don’t match the prior period’s closing balances. The carry-forward is showing wrong figures, ghost transactions, or unexpected variance from day 1.

What this usually means

One of three things:

  • The prior period closed with a variance that was carried forward as residual. Check the prior period’s Inventory Validation indicator — if it was red at close, it carried that variance forward.
  • A late transaction landed in the prior period after close, and the system is showing that delta as the difference between closing and opening. Check JDE for any backdated transactions in the prior period.
  • The new period’s data hasn’t fully refreshed yet. RapidReconciler imports from JDE on a schedule. If you’re looking at the new period within minutes of close, the import may not have caught up yet.

What to check

  1. Look at the prior period’s Validation indicator — if it’s red, the prior period’s variance is the source.
  2. Compare the prior period’s closing balance and the new period’s opening balance side by side. The difference itself often tells you what happened (e.g., a single transaction landing late).
  3. Check JDE for any backdated activity in the prior period after the close timestamp.
  4. Wait for the next refresh cycle if you just closed. Give it the customary refresh interval (usually 24 hours, but ask your RR Administrator for your specific cadence) before treating it as a real discrepancy.
  5. Hard-reload the page with Shift+F5 — sometimes the carry-forward UI is showing a stale calculation.

If figures still don’t reconcile after a refresh

Click the red Inventory Validation indicator on the new period (if it’s red) to export the variance Excel. The Excel will identify which specific transactions are causing the carry-forward mismatch, which is what RR support needs to diagnose further.

If the symptoms include a red System Status light alongside the carry-forward variance, see the System status light is red scenario — the IT-side environment checks (disk, TempDB, SQL Agent, SSIS) often resolve the paired condition.

!

If you’ve checked all of the above and the carry-forward still doesn’t reconcile, see Email RR Support.

Email RR Support

Period-close issues are time-sensitive. The more context you include up front, the less back-and-forth before we can help. Email rrsupport@getgsi.com.

What to include

  • The period you’re trying to close, and the period selector value when the issue happens.
  • What you’re seeing — the close button greyed out, an error, a red status light, missing dropdown option, etc. Screenshots ideal.
  • The status of both indicators — Inventory Validation (green / red) and System Status (green / red).
  • Whether the GL batches in the prior period are confirmed posted in JDE.
  • The variance Excel if a Validation light or System Status light is red.
  • Whether other users at your site are affected or it’s just you.
  • The variance amount (if applicable) and which periods it spans.
