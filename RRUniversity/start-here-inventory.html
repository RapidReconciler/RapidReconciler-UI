Start Here — Inventory

A wayfinding guide for inventory accountants and cost accountants new to RapidReconciler. JD Edwards experience is assumed throughout. Warehouse personnel responsible for transfer orders should read Start Here — Warehouse Transfers instead.

Last reviewed: January 2026 Reading time: ~15 minutes Press Ctrl+F (or ⌘+F) to search this page

Who This Is For

You are responsible for some or all of the inventory reconciliation cycle in JD Edwards: monitoring variances during the period, investigating cardex and account discrepancies, and posting closing entries at period end. RapidReconciler is the tool that surfaces what needs your attention and tells you where to look in JDE.

This guide does not teach JDE. It assumes you already know what F4111 is, what a DMAAI does, and how a perpetual inventory system reconciles to the GL. What it does is map that JDE knowledge to RapidReconciler’s vocabulary, screens, and workflow — and tell you what to read, in what order, in your first month.

What “Ready” Looks Like

Use these milestones to gauge your own progress. They are not prescriptive timelines — some sites move faster, some take longer — but they are the typical learning arc for a JDE-experienced inventory user.

Day 1

Logged in. Both status lights green. Can identify your accounts in the filter and select the period you care about.

Week 1

Can read the Valuation section and explain what each variance source (Carry Forward, GL Batches, End of Day, Transactions, Cardex, Manual JEs) means and where it comes from.

Month 1

Has run a full period-end close at least once with supervision. Has produced and saved an Audit Report. Has reviewed Integrity Reports 2 through 6.

What RapidReconciler Does (and Doesn’t)

Five minutes of orientation. The aim is to translate what you already know about JDE into where it lives and what it’s called inside RapidReconciler — and to set boundaries on what RapidReconciler can and cannot change.

The Read-Only Model

RapidReconciler does not write to JDE. It reads from JDE on a nightly import cycle, compares the data, and surfaces discrepancies. Every correction — every IA, every JE, every DMAAI fix — is posted in JD Edwards using the tools you already know. RapidReconciler then picks up the change at the next nightly refresh.

RapidReconciler can changeOnly JD Edwards can change
Notes attached to a transaction or order; suspensions and exclusions on PO Receipts; filter and view preferences; report exports. F4111, F41021, F0902, F0911, DMAAI tables, item master records, GL account balances — everything that matters for a reconciliation.

The practical implication: if you spot a problem in RapidReconciler at 2pm and post a corrective JE in JDE at 3pm, the variance will not clear in RapidReconciler until the following morning’s import. Plan investigations and corrections accordingly.

What It’s Comparing

The headline question RapidReconciler answers for inventory is one you already know how to ask: does the perpetual ledger agree to the general ledger for this period?

RapidReconciler FieldJDE SourceWhat It Represents
GL Balance F0902 The general ledger period-end balance for the inventory account.
Perpetual Balance F4111 summarized Sum of every cardex transaction posted to that account in that period.
Out of Balance Calculated The difference. Zero means the perpetual agrees to the GL.

If the Out of Balance is non-zero, the Variance Calculation section breaks the difference into six attributable sources (Carry Forward, GL Batches, End of Day, Transactions, Cardex, Manual JEs). Each one points to a specific kind of JDE-side correction. Most of your day-to-day work will be working those six lines down to zero.

If you have RR access, log in and find the Reconciliation page. Pick one of your accounts and one closed period. Compare the GL Balance in RR to the same account and period in JDE. They should match exactly — if they don’t, your filter or period is off, not the data.

Your First Session

A 10-minute guided first sit-down at the Reconciliation page. The aim is not to do real work — it’s to confirm you can find each landmark and read what it says. If your Out of Balance is non-zero at the end, that’s expected. Investigation comes later.

Before You Start

You need a RapidReconciler login (provided by your administrator) and a browser. JDE access is not required for this session — you’re only looking, not posting.

The 10-Minute Walk

#ActionWhat To Look For
1 Log in. You land on the Reconciliation page by default. Bookmark this URL for later.
2 Find the two status lights. Top center of the page. Inventory Validation on the left, System Status on the right. Both should be green. If either is red or yellow, hover for a tooltip explaining why — do not act on the data until both are green.
3 Open the period selector. Top right corner. Pick the most recently closed period (not the current open one). Closed periods are stable; the current period changes daily.
4 Apply your account filter. Left side of the page, four columns: Company → Business Unit → Object → Subsidiary. Select the company and account combination you’re responsible for. The filter applies left-to-right; removing a company also removes its child entities.
5 Read the Valuation section. Three numbers: GL Balance, Perpetual Balance, Out of Balance. The GL Balance should match what you see in JDE for the same account and period. If it doesn’t, your filter or period is off — the numbers come from JDE, so they will agree if you’re asking the same question.
6 Glance at the Variance Calculation section. Below Valuation. Six lines, one per variance source. The non-zero ones are where the Out of Balance comes from. Don’t investigate yet — just see them.
7 Click the Out of Balance History Graph. Shows variance trends across the last 14 periods. Tells you at a glance whether your accounts run clean or have a chronic issue. Click a data point to jump to that period.
8 Log out, take a break. You’ve seen the page. The next session is when you start asking why the variance lines are non-zero.
i

If something doesn’t look right — for example, the GL Balance differs from JDE by a large amount, or the period selector is empty — stop and ask your administrator. These usually indicate a configuration or permission issue, not a real reconciliation problem.

Try this now

The 10-minute walk above is meant to be done, not just read. If you have RR access, work through the eight steps in your own application before continuing to the next topic.

Don’t aim for understanding everything you see — aim for being able to find each landmark. If you got stuck on any step, that’s the part to ask your administrator about.

The Five Variance Sources at a Glance

Each non-zero line in the Variance Calculation section corresponds to a different kind of discrepancy with its own corrective procedure. This table is a routing map: when you see a non-zero variance on your account, it tells you what the source likely means and where to find the full procedure for clearing it. The table doesn’t replace reading the procedure — it points you at the right one.

Variance Source What It Likely Means Where to Find the Procedure
Carry Forward A prior-period variance was left unresolved. The amount carried into this period and is still open. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 2.2 (Carry Forward)
GL Batches One or more GL batches haven’t posted — usually approval holds, posting errors, or missing batch headers. Must be $0 before close. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 2.2 (GL Batches)
End of Day Item ledger records exist but no GL batch has been created yet — usually because nightly Sales Update or Manufacturing Accounting jobs haven’t run. Must be $0 before close. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 2.2 (End of Day)
Transactions Dollar differences between item ledger and GL entries that didn’t match on company, account, period, or document. The corrective action is always an offsetting manual JE in JDE. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 2.2 (Transactions)
Cardex Items where the summarized item ledger doesn’t match the on-hand balance. Either a quantity variance (needs IT SQL correction) or an amount variance (cleared via dollars-only IA in JDE). Handling Cardex Variance — the dedicated walkthrough
i

About Manual Journal Entries. A sixth line on the Variance Calculation section, Manual Journal Entries, surfaces manual entries posted directly to the inventory GL account. This is informational rather than investigational — the line shows that manual entries are accounted for in the variance math, not that something is wrong. No corrective action.

Stop and Ask If…

Three signals that mean stop reading and find a colleague or your administrator. None of these are failures — they’re the questions experienced users still ask each other.

i

The GL Balance in RapidReconciler doesn’t match what JDE shows. The numbers come from JDE. Disagreement means your filter, your period, or your account selection is off. Don’t investigate variances until the GL number agrees with JDE.

i

The status light is yellow and won’t go green. A flashing yellow System Status means the import is still running. If it’s been running for hours, contact your administrator — this is an operational issue, not a user one.

i

You can’t see the accounts you expected. Account permissions are configured per user. If your filter list is missing a company or business unit you should see, ask your administrator before working around it.

Your First Period-End Close

The transition from monitoring variances to closing and posting. The procedure is fully documented in the Reconciliation Walkthrough — Step 3 and Step 4. This topic covers the things around the procedure: how most sites approach a first close, and the three mistakes that bite new users hardest.

Two Recommendations Before You Close Live

Neither of these is enforced by the application. Both are worth the time.

Pair With an Experienced Reviewer for the First Close

Have someone who has closed before sit with you (or be on a call) for your first end-to-end period close. Their job is not to do the work — it is to catch the small judgment calls that the documentation can’t capture, and to confirm you’re reading what you think you’re reading. Most failures on a first close are not procedural mistakes; they are confidence mistakes (acting on data that isn’t ready, or hesitating on data that is).

Run One Mock Close in a Non-Production Environment

If your organization has a non-production RapidReconciler environment with reasonably current data, run a complete close against a prior period before doing it live. Walk through Steps 1 through 4 end-to-end, post a mock JE, produce the Audit Report. Going through the motions once with no consequences is the single highest-leverage thing you can do to be ready for a real close.

If a non-production environment isn’t available, the next best option is to walk through a closed prior period in production with your reviewer — reading what was done without changing anything — so you understand what a successful close looks like before producing your own.

Pre-Close Checklist

Before you post the closing journal entry, the four pre-close conditions in Walkthrough Step 3.1 must be satisfied. Repeating them here for emphasis:

  • Pre-close requirements
  • GL Batches variance = $0.
  • End of Day variance = $0.
  • Transaction variances reviewed; offsetting JEs prepared.
  • Carry Forward accounted for in the manual entry (if applicable).

If any of those four are not met, do not proceed. The Walkthrough explains why each one matters.

Top Three New-User Mistakes

Drawn from the Pitfalls section of the Reconciliation Walkthrough, narrowed to the three that specifically catch first-time closers.

!

1. Posting on stale data. If either status light is red or yellow, the data is not ready. Acting on it bakes a variance into the period that you’ll have to chase next month as Carry Forward. The single most common new-user mistake is treating “the lights will go green soon” as a green light. Wait.

!

2. Skipping Carry Forward investigation because the amount is small. A small Carry Forward this month becomes a slightly larger Carry Forward next month, and the cause becomes harder to trace each period. Resolve in the period it originates whenever possible. If you accept it into your manual JE for materiality reasons, document the decision in the audit trail — future-you will need to know.

!

3. Not saving the Audit Report externally. The Audit Report is the permanent record of the period’s reconciliation. RapidReconciler will not retain detail data forever — purges happen on schedule. After every close, export the Audit Report to your team’s document repository and confirm it’s there before considering the close complete.

If Your First Close Has a Non-Zero Out of Balance After Posting

It happens. The Audit Report tells you what was posted and what the balances looked like at the moment of posting. Review it with your buddy. Most causes fall into one of four buckets:

  • A variance line was non-zero at post time and got rolled into the JE rather than being investigated.
  • The post-JE nightly refresh hadn’t completed when the Out of Balance was checked — check again the next morning.
  • An additional transaction posted in JDE between the close and the import, creating new activity outside the closed period.
  • Carry Forward from the prior period wasn’t accounted for in the closing JE.

None of these is a failure of the close itself. They’re learning events. Document what happened in your team’s notes — the second close benefits enormously from the first one’s post-mortem.

See Inventory Reconciliation Walkthrough for the full close procedure (Steps 3 and 4) and the complete Pitfalls list.

Vocabulary

RapidReconciler-specific terms that aren’t standard JDE. Each entry is a one-line definition plus a link to the doc that explains it in depth. Skim once for awareness; come back when a term shows up on screen and you want to remember what it meant.

As-Of page
The supporting detail behind the Perpetual Balance — lets you view item/branch/location/lot balances at any single date in the period. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Reference Material.
Audit Report
The permanent record of a period’s reconciliation. Contains accounts summary, unposted batches, end-of-day items, manual JEs, variances, and perpetual detail. Save externally after every close. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 3.3.
Cardex Integrity pop-up
The view that lists items where summarized F4111 doesn’t agree to F41021. Tells you whether the variance is quantity, amount, or both. Handling Cardex Variance.
Carry Forward variance
The portion of this period’s Out of Balance that was unresolved in the prior period. If left untreated it accumulates and compounds over time.
End of Day variance
Item ledger records (F4111) that don’t yet have a batch number or GL date because Sales Update or Manufacturing Accounting hasn’t run. Must be $0 before period close.
GL Batches variance
Unposted GL detail entries (F0911). Must be $0 before period close.
Integrity Reports (2–6)
Six monthly-review reports surfacing JDE configuration issues that cause future variances: DMAAI Entry, Excluded GL Classes, UOM Conversion, GL Class Integrity, Frozen Cost. Reviewed monthly, not just when something is wrong. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 4.
Manual Journal Entries (variance line)
Manual entries posted directly to the inventory GL account, surfaced in the variance calculation so they’re visible inside the reconciliation. No corrective action required — the line is informational.
Offset Account widget (star icon)
Period-end close tool. Hover over End of Day or Transactions to see the inventory account and suggested offset for each variance, exportable to Excel for pasting into a JDE JE. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Step 2.3.
Out of Balance
The headline number on the Reconciliation page. GL Balance minus Perpetual Balance. Zero means the perpetual agrees to the GL for the selected period and accounts.
Out of Balance History Graph
The 14-period trend chart on the Reconciliation page. The fastest way to tell whether a variance is recurring or a one-off.
Reconciliation page
The default landing page after login. Status lights, period selector, account filters, Valuation section, and Variance Calculation section all live here.
Roll Forward page
Item activity across a date range, mapped to administrator-configured columns. Informational — not part of the standard reconciliation flow. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Reference Material.
Status lights
Two indicators at the top center of the Reconciliation page. Inventory Validation confirms prior-period carry-forward is accurate; System Status confirms the latest JDE import succeeded. Both must be green before posting any GL adjustments.
Tolerance Adjust
A line on the Offset Account export representing sub-unit rounding. Replace with the correct GL account before pasting into JDE.
Transactions page
Used when the Transactions line in the Variance Calculation is non-zero. Lists documents where F4111 doesn’t match F0911. Reconciliation Walkthrough → Reference Material.
Valuation section
The three-number block on the Reconciliation page: GL Balance, Perpetual Balance, Out of Balance. Answers “does perpetual match the GL for this period?”
Variance Calculation section
Below Valuation. Six attributable lines (Carry Forward, GL Batches, End of Day, Transactions, Cardex, Manual JEs). The non-zero ones are where Out of Balance comes from.
