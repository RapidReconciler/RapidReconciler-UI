I can’t sign in

Several different login failures land here. Match what you’re seeing to one of the sections below; each has its own fix.

“Invalid email or password”

Most likely your password has expired (RapidReconciler rotates passwords every 90 days) or you’ve mistyped. Reset it:

  1. On the login screen, click Forgot Your Password? below the password field.
  2. Enter the email address tied to your RapidReconciler account.
  3. Click Reset.
  4. You’ll receive an email with a reset link — check your spam folder if it doesn’t arrive within a few minutes.
  5. Follow the link to set a new password.

Account locked after multiple failed attempts

RapidReconciler will temporarily lock your account after several failed login attempts in a row. Two ways forward:

  • Wait — the lockout clears automatically after a short period, then use Forgot Your Password? to reset.
  • Contact your RR Administrator — they can unlock your account immediately from the user-management screen.

Email address not recognized

If the system says your email isn’t recognized, you may not yet be set up in RapidReconciler — or your address may be different from what was used to create your account.

  • Confirm with your RR Administrator that your account exists in Admin → Users.
  • Confirm the exact email address used. RapidReconciler matches the address exactly — jane.doe@example.com is not the same as jdoe@example.com.

Connectivity / server errors

If the error mentions a connection or backend timeout (often referencing an Azure host like *.cloudapp.net), “server unavailable,” or any other network-side message that isn’t a credentials issue, your IT needs to look at it.

The fastest diagnostic: try logging in from a different network (a phone hotspot is fine). If it works elsewhere, the problem is on your corporate network. If it fails everywhere, the problem is on the GSI side.

For a step-by-step runbook with a pre-filled email to IT, see the I can’t log in scenario.

If none of the above resolves it — or your error doesn’t match any of them — see Email RR Support for what to send.

Browser shows “Not Secure”

You reach rapidreconciler.getgsi.com but the browser displays a security warning, a “Not Secure” banner in the address bar, or a full warning page asking you to confirm before continuing.

What this means

The site’s SSL certificate (the trust mechanism that secures the connection) has either expired, is misconfigured, or your browser cached an older version of it. This is not something you or your IT can fix — only GSI can refresh and redeploy the certificate.

Quick checks before contacting support

  1. Click the warning icon in the address bar and open the certificate details. Note the expiry date and the issuer’s common name — you’ll include these in your support email.
  2. Hard-reload the browser — Shift+F5 on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac. If GSI just rolled out a renewed certificate, your browser may be serving you a cached old one.
  3. Try in a private / incognito window — this bypasses cached certificate state.

For a more detailed walkthrough including what to attach when emailing support, see the browser-not-secure scenario.

!

If hard-reloading and a private window don’t resolve it, see Email RR Support.

Blank screen after login

You log in successfully — the credentials worked — but pages are blank, no figures display, or the screen looks empty. Common phrasings: “no data,” “everything is empty,” “the page is blank,” “no menu items.” The cause is almost always something on your network, browser, or VPN — not on the GSI side.

First, ask: is it just you?

Try the same login on a different computer (a coworker’s, your home machine, a phone-tethered laptop), or have a coworker try the same page on theirs:

  • Works elsewhere → the issue is your laptop. Steps below address this.
  • Fails elsewhere too → the issue is your network or company-side. Steps 4 and 5 below are most likely to help.

Try these steps in order

  1. Connect to VPN if you’re working remotely. RapidReconciler is only reachable from your corporate network or via your organization’s VPN.
  2. Hard-reload the browser with Shift+F5 on Windows or Cmd+Shift+R on Mac. If that doesn’t help, fully close all browser windows and reopen.
  3. Allow local-network access in your browser. Edge and Chrome have a permission gate; if rapidreconciler.getgsi.com isn’t allowed, all data calls silently fail. Edge: Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Site permissions → All permissions → Local network access → ensure the URL is in “Allowed.” Chrome has the equivalent under its site-permissions area.
  4. Clear cookies and cached files for rapidreconciler.getgsi.com only (your IT can help). Close the browser fully, reopen, log in fresh.
  5. Check with your IT for recent network or security changes — new web filter, DNS filter, secure web gateway, endpoint security policy, Windows update, VPN config change. If multiple users at your site are affected at once, this is the most likely cause.

For the full step-by-step runbook including IT-conversation hints (specific products to ask about, the cross-network test, what to whitelist), see the no-data-after-login scenario.

!

Still blank after working through Steps 1–5? See Email RR Support.

Menu items are missing

You can log in but the left-side navigation is missing items you used to see, or items you expected based on your role.

This is a permissions issue, not a bug

RapidReconciler shows different menu items to different users based on the permissions granted by your RR Administrator — the person at your company who manages user accounts. If you’re missing a menu item, it’s almost always because that permission isn’t set on your account yet.

What to do

  1. Identify your RR Administrator — usually someone in finance or IT who handles user accounts in RapidReconciler.
  2. Tell them which menu item you’re missing and what you’re trying to do (e.g., “I need to see the In Transit module to reconcile transfer orders”).
  3. Your Administrator can adjust your permissions in Admin → Users; the change is immediate.
  4. After they confirm, refresh the company-selector widget on your page or log out and back in for the new menu items to appear.

If your RR Administrator says the permissions are correct but you still don’t see the menu items, your browser session may be holding a cached older permission set. Log out completely and log back in. See the newly-granted-companies scenario for a fuller walkthrough.

Email RR Support

If the steps in the topics above didn’t resolve your issue, email rrsupport@getgsi.com. The more context you include, the faster we can help.

What to include

  • What you’re seeing — the exact error message wording, or what the screen shows. A screenshot is ideal.
  • What you tried — which steps from this page or a linked scenario you walked, and what happened on each.
  • When it started — today only, since a recent change, etc.
  • Scope — is it just you, multiple users at your company, or just one machine? For the “blank screen” case, this is the highest-value clue we can get.
  • Anything that changed recently — new corporate web filter, VPN change, browser update, password rotation, etc.
