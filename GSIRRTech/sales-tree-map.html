What this is. A complete map of the GSIRRSales documentation set — every page, every cross-link, every anchor target. The whole tree fits inside the GSIRRSales/ folder; unlike the tech corpus, sales has zero cross-folder references. The trade-off is that the corpus uses four distinct page templates (workflow, playbook, marketing-A, marketing-B), and the non-workflow templates do not back-link to the section root.

How to read it. The tree below shows the hub-to-leaf navigation paths. The card grid further down breaks each page into its sections and outbound links in detail. The insights panel at the bottom captures the structural patterns worth knowing — and a separate flow-consistency audit covering semantic mismatches between pages (step numbering, attendance, document-handoff sequencing).

Patterns & observations

  • The sales tree is broader and flatter than tech. Hub → workflow → leaf, with twelve pages all at one level under the workflow root. There are almost no chains — only rr-discovery-call.html reaches across to rr-provisioning.html and rr-installation-prep.html. Otherwise each leaf is a terminus.
  • Four template archetypes coexist in one folder. The workflow template (workflow root + start-here + proof-of-concept) uses Source Sans 3 / JetBrains Mono with a topnav and section-nav. The playbook template (discovery-call, provisioning, installation-prep, contract, MSA, SOW) uses an embedded base64 logo, a sidebar nav, and an "Internal Use" badge — and crucially has no topnav and no back-link. The two marketing templates (self-guided-tour vs. overview/fact-sheet) use different fonts again. This corpus is far less unified than the tech corpus.
  • Most pages have no way home. Once you're on a playbook page or a marketing page, the only path back to sales-client-management.html is the browser back button — there is no brand link, no breadcrumb, no nav. This is the single biggest navigation issue in the sales corpus, but it's a content-design decision rather than a link bug, so the audit did not modify it.
  • The "multiple H1" finding split into two real categories. The contract template (2 H1) and SOW template (3 H1) were genuine misuse — flat documents with sub-section H1s — and were downgraded to H2. Provisioning (10 H1) and installation-prep (8 H1) turned out to be single-page apps where each <section class="view"> is a separately-displayed virtual page; their multiple H1s are correct and were left alone. Self-guided-tour's two hero H1s were left alone per user direction.
  • Three pages exchange contract signal. The three workflow-template pages all had identical Contact Support nav-cta buttons stripped per the standing rule. The other nine pages don't have a topnav at all, so there was nothing to strip.
  • Sales has zero cross-folder references. Unlike tech (which had three out-of-folder links — two to ../MiscDocs/ and one to ../GSIRRSales/), sales lives entirely inside GSIRRSales/. The only external destinations are www.getgsi.com, rapidreconciler.getgsi.com, and one tel: link.
  • Two templates appear orphaned from this corpus. rr-msa-template.html and rr-sow-template.html are not linked from any of the 12 sales pages. User confirms they are referenced from elsewhere (presumably the contract template's downstream legal toolkit or a separate compliance index).
  • No cross-folder bridges in either direction. An earlier version of this map noted a tech→sales bridge — ../GSIRRTech/start-here-monitor.html linking into rr-installation-prep.html — but the subsequent tech-side flow-consistency audit found that link was pointing at the wrong file (the email sample described in the surrounding sentence actually lives in the tech folder's installing-production-database.html) and redirected it. After both audits, neither folder references the other.

Flow-consistency audit

  • Two pages did not self-identify their step number. Discovery-call and proof-of-concept correctly told the reader "Step 6" and "Step 2A" via their eyebrows. Provisioning and installation-prep did not — readers arriving from a link had no idea where they sat in the flow. Both eyebrows now read Sales Process · Step N · Document Name.
  • Discovery-call attendance disagreed with the workflow root. The workflow root's Step 6 aria-label and figcaption named only RR DBA as the technical participant, but the discovery-call playbook describes RR DBA and RR Network Tech with separate prep checklists for each. Workflow root updated to match the playbook (which had the more accurate description of who actually does the work).
  • Discovery-call expected a "reply to the provisioning doc" that doesn't exist. The playbook told prep'ing teams to review the customer's "reply" to the Provisioning Document, including a Configuration 1/2/3 selection — but rr-provisioning.html has no submission mechanism and the actual topology selection is captured in rr-installation-prep.html, which isn't sent until after the discovery call. Reworded so the topology is "discussed on the call · finalized in install-prep submission" — matches the actual documented flow.
  • The install meeting was real but undocumented in the workflow. Install-prep and discovery-call both reference an "install meeting" — a 2-3 hour event GSI schedules after the customer submits their install-prep email. The workflow root's SVG jumped straight from Step 7 (send doc) to Step 8 (customer emails support) → "Customer Live", with no acknowledgment of the meeting. Added a small italic annotation between Step 7 and Step 8 (matching the existing "RR DBA joins call" annotation style) and extended the figcaption to mention the scheduled meeting. Step numbering preserved to avoid cascading rewrites.
  • One copy typo: "15 of buffer for Q&A" → "15 minutes of buffer for Q&A" in discovery-call's glance card. The same line was correct in the body paragraph below.
