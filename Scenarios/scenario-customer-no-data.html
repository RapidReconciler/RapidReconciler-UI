Blank or missing data

No data visible after logging in

You can sign in, but the page is blank or shows no figures. The cause is almost always something on your network, browser, or VPN — the steps below resolve most cases without contacting support.

What you’re seeing

  • You log in successfully, but the Reconciliation, Inventory, or other pages are empty.
  • The screen shows “I see no data,” “the page is blank,” or “everything is empty.”
  • Admin → General displays No database connection. Delete RapidReconciler. Cardex requires a database connection.
  • It started suddenly with no apparent change on your end.
  • It affects multiple users at the same time, or the opposite — only you, while coworkers are unaffected.
  • Common phrasings: “blank screen on my laptop,” “works for everyone else but me,” “logs in fine on a different computer.”
Quick triage

First, ask: is it just you, or are coworkers seeing it too? The answer changes which steps below are most likely to help.

  • Only you — or only this machine. Try logging in on a different computer (a coworker’s, your own home machine, or a phone-tethered laptop), or have a coworker try the same page on theirs. If RR works elsewhere, the issue is isolated to your laptop — Steps 2–4 are most likely to help, plus Step 5 (asking IT to look at this specific machine).
  • Multiple users at once — this is more likely a network or company-side change than your machine. Step 1 (VPN) and Step 5 (asking IT about recent web-filter, DNS, or firewall changes) are the high-value picks.

Try these steps in order

Step 1
Confirm you’re on VPN or the corporate network
When this applies: you’re working remotely — from home, while travelling, or off-site.

RapidReconciler is only reachable from your corporate network or via your organization’s VPN. If you’re disconnected, login can succeed but no data will flow.

  1. If you’re working remotely, confirm you’re connected to your organization’s VPN or approved remote-access method.
  2. If you believe you’re connected, disconnect and reconnect the VPN, then hard-refresh the browser.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Hard-reload the browser
When this applies: you’re confirmed on the corporate network or VPN.

A stale browser cache or a partially-failed initial page load is the most common simple cause.

  1. Press Shift+F5 on Windows or Cmd+Shift+R on Mac.
  2. If that doesn’t resolve it, fully close all browser windows, reopen, and log in again.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 3.
Step 3
Allow local-network access in your browser
When this applies: your browser was recently updated, or this is a new workstation.

Edge and Chrome have a permission gate that controls whether sites can connect to local-network devices. If RapidReconciler isn’t on the allowed list, all data calls silently fail.

  1. In Edge: open Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Site permissions → All permissions → Local network access.
  2. Under “Allowed to connect to any device on your local network,” verify rapidreconciler.getgsi.com is present.
  3. If it’s missing, click Add site and add it.
  4. Hard-reload the page and check whether data appears. (Chrome has the equivalent setting under its site-permissions area.)

If you can’t find or change the setting on your machine, ask your IT department to adjust it.

Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 4.
Step 4
Clear cookies and cached files for the site
When this applies: Steps 1–3 are done and your IT has confirmed no firewall changes.

Stale or corrupted authentication tokens, cookies, or service-worker state can hold the page in a broken state even after the underlying issue is gone.

  1. Ask your IT for browser-specific instructions to clear cookies and cached files for rapidreconciler.getgsi.com only (not all sites).
  2. Close the browser fully, reopen, and log in again with a fresh session.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 5.
Step 5
Check with your IT for recent network, security, or local-machine changes
When this applies: the issue started suddenly, multiple users are affected at the same time, Admin → General specifically shows the “no database connection” message, or Steps 1–4 didn’t fix a single-user / single-machine issue.

If multiple users are hit at once, a new web filter, DNS filter, secure web gateway, or endpoint-security policy is the most common cause. Ask your IT contact:

  • Has anything been turned on, updated, or changed recently for web filtering, DNS filtering, secure web gateways, or endpoint security? Common products: Cisco Umbrella, Zscaler, Cloudflare Gateway, Forcepoint, Palo Alto/Prisma, Fortinet, SonicWall, CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Microsoft Defender.
  • Was there a Windows update, a new VPN client, or a switch from split-tunnel to full-tunnel VPN?
  • Are other *.getgsi.com domains also blocked — e.g., a related GSI SharePoint site? If yes, the web-filter rule is scoped broader than just RR.

Then run a quick cross-network test: try RapidReconciler from a different network (a mobile hotspot is fine). If it works from a different network, the issue is on your corporate network — ask your IT to whitelist rapidreconciler.getgsi.com on whichever product they identified, then hard-refresh the browser.

If only you are affected and RR worked on a different computer, the issue is isolated to your laptop. Ask your IT to check:

  • Local firewall on the affected workstation.
  • Browser policy or Windows Group Policy that might restrict site permissions or local-network access.
  • DNS settings on this specific machine: custom DNS server, hosts file, browser DNS-over-HTTPS.
  • Whether this workstation has a different security profile from coworkers’ (newer endpoint agent, isolated VLAN, etc.).
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue below to email RR support.

Still not resolved?

Email RapidReconciler support

Send an email to rrsupport@getgsi.com with as much of the following as you can:

  • Which steps above you tried, and what happened on each.
  • A screenshot of the page where data is missing — Admin → General is especially helpful if it shows the “no database connection” message.
  • Whether one user is affected or multiple, and roughly when it started.
  • Whether the issue follows the user (works on a different machine on the same network) or follows the network (fails on the same machine when on a different network).
  • If your IT investigated, what they found and what they ruled out.
