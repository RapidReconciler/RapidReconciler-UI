Blank or missing data

User sees no data after login

You can sign in, but the page is blank or shows no figures. The cause is almost always something on your network, browser, or VPN — the steps below resolve most cases without contacting support.

What you’re seeing

  • You log in successfully, but the Reconciliation, Inventory, or other pages are empty.
  • The screen shows “I see no data,” “the page is blank,” or “everything is empty.”
  • Common phrasings: “blank screen on my laptop,” “works for everyone else but me,” “logs in fine on a different computer.”

Try these steps in order

Step 1
Confirm you’re on VPN or the corporate network
When this applies: you’re working remotely — from home, while travelling, or off-site.

RapidReconciler is only reachable from your corporate network or via your organization’s VPN. If you’re disconnected, login can succeed but no data will flow.

  • If you’re working remotely, confirm you’re connected to your organization’s VPN or approved remote-access method.
  • If you believe you’re connected, disconnect and reconnect the VPN, then hard-refresh the browser.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Allow local-network access in your browser
When this applies: your browser was recently updated, or this is a new workstation.

Edge and Chrome have a permission gate that controls whether sites can connect to local-network devices. If RapidReconciler isn’t on the allowed list, all data calls silently fail.

  • In Edge: open Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Site permissions → All permissions → Local network access.
  • Under “Allowed to connect to any device on your local network,” verify rapidreconciler.getgsi.com is present.
  • If it’s missing, click Add site and add it.
  • Hard-reload the page and check whether data appears. (Chrome has the equivalent setting under its site-permissions area.)

If you can’t find or change the setting on your machine, ask your IT department to adjust it.

Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 3.
Step 3
Check with coworkers
When this applies: Steps 1 and 2 didn’t fix it.

Got your answer? Continue to Step 4.
Step 4
Contact your IT and share what you’ve found For your IT
When this applies: Steps 1–3 didn’t fix it.

You won’t be able to fix this yourself — it’s a network, machine, or server issue your IT needs to investigate.
