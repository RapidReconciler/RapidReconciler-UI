Cardex variance and red status light after month-end

The System Status indicator is red and the Cardex balance hasn’t cleared after month-end — often with a noticeable variance, end-of-day transactions not settling to zero, and the next accounting period missing from the dropdown. The most common cause is a single problematic order line introducing a duplication error in RapidReconciler processing. Your IT or DBA can isolate the line, remove it from RR, and let JDE repopulate cleanly.

Looking for a different red-status-light cause? See System status light is red — the IT-side checklist (disk, TempDB, SQL Agent / SSIS) covers most other causes when there’s no Cardex / month-end pattern.

What you’re seeing

  • The System Status indicator is red — status shows “failed.”
  • The Cardex balance hasn’t cleared after month-end (often a sizable variance — tens of thousands of dollars).
  • End-of-day transactions aren’t settling to zero.
  • The next accounting period (e.g., the new month just started) isn’t available in the period dropdown.
  • The RR site may be unresponsive or have login issues during the failure window.
  • Common phrasings: “Cardex variance not clearing,” “month-end isn’t closing,” “system status failed after month-end,” “new period missing.”

Recommendations to share with your IT

✉
All steps below are for your IT
This scenario is fully IT-side. Click to draft an email with the steps below pre-filled, ready to forward to your IT department or DBA.
Step 1
Confirm RR service is up before investigating data

If RR is unresponsive or login is broken, that’s a service-side issue and the data investigation will be premature.

  1. Confirm the RR site loads and login works.
  2. If the site is still unresponsive, wait for the service to recover (or restart it) before continuing — service problems can mimic the data symptoms.
  3. Once the site is responsive, confirm the System Status indicator is showing “failed” with the date / time.
Site responsive and System Status confirmed red? Continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Isolate the duplicated transaction

The blocker is almost always a single order line that’s been duplicated inside RapidReconciler processing.

  • Review transaction-level data in the Cardex / F4111 context.
  • Look for stuck or duplicated order lines that line up in time with when the batch failed.
  • Confirm the duplication is in RR — not in the base JDE data. JDE itself should look clean. If JDE also has the duplicate, the issue is upstream and the fix is different (escalate to RR support before changing anything).
Specific duplicated line identified in RR only? Continue to Step 3.
Step 3
Remove the problematic transaction from RR — let JDE repopulate

Don’t manually patch the values. The safe pattern is: remove from RR, then let JDE rebuild on its next sync.

  1. Remove the duplicated transaction from RR.
  2. Let JDE repopulate the data cleanly on the next refresh.
RR data cleaned and JDE confirmed repopulating? Continue to Step 4.
Step 4
Re-run the RR batch job
  1. Execute the RR batch job manually after the data correction.
  2. Monitor it — the job should now progress past the previous failure point.
Job runs to completion? Continue to Step 5 to validate.
Step 5
Validate across multiple accounting periods

“No variance” in the current period isn’t enough — check the surrounding periods before declaring the issue resolved.

  • Confirm the job completed successfully and updated data is visible in the UI.
  • Check the variance across periods:
    • Current period may still show a residual variance — that’s expected during processing.
    • The future period (e.g., the new month) should reflect ongoing variance correctly.
  • Pop-up detail views may still show transaction-level discrepancies — this is expected, not a failure.
  • Have a user confirm functionality from the UI side: data refreshed, system status restored, Cardex behaving normally.
Validated across periods and user confirms? Resolved.

Still not resolved?

Email RapidReconciler support

If your IT can’t isolate the duplicated order line, finds the duplication in JDE itself (not just RR), or removing the line doesn’t restore the batch, email rrsupport@getgsi.com with:

  • Which steps your IT walked, and what they found at each.
  • Where the duplication was identified (Cardex / F4111 details, the order / line numbers).
  • Whether the duplication was found in RR only, or also present in JDE.
  • The variance amount and the affected period(s).
  • Whether the future period is still missing from the dropdown.
  • A screenshot of the Reconciliation page header showing the red System Status, plus the variance calculation panel if visible.
