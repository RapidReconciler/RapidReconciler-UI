Inventory validation light is red

The Inventory Validation indicator at the top of the Reconciliation page is red or amber (anything other than green), often with an “out of balance” or variance message. The most common cause — by far — is unposted GL batches in the prior period. Most cases resolve once those batches are posted in JDE.

Don’t start your month-end close until both status lights are green. RapidReconciler is read-only by design — it diagnoses the variance for you, but corrections happen in JDE. Walk Step 1 first; if all batches are posted and the indicator is still off-color, escalate to RR support before proceeding with close.

What you’re seeing

  • The Inventory Validation indicator at the top of the Reconciliation page is red.
  • An “out of balance” or variance message is displayed.
  • The variance calculation panel shows a carry-forward amount that matches an unreconciled variance.
  • Common phrasings: “the red light came on,” “this just popped up,” or “we have a $X variance for period.”

Try these steps in order

Step 1
Read the indicator’s tooltip

Hover over the red Inventory Validation indicator. The tooltip explains the most common cause:

“This indicator is red if the roll-forward from the prior period fails. This is most likely due to one or more unposted GL batches. If all batches in the prior period have been posted, please contact us at rrsupport@getgsi.com for assistance.”

Note the period (e.g., April 2024) and the variance amount.

Read it? Continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Check JDE for unposted GL batches in the prior period
When this applies: always check this first — it resolves roughly 80% of cases.

This is a check inside your JDE ERP, not RapidReconciler.

  1. In JDE, look for any unposted GL batches in the prior period that affects this validation.
  2. If you find unposted batches, post them.
  3. Refresh the Reconciliation page in RapidReconciler and check whether the indicator goes green.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 3.
Step 3
Export the variance Excel report
When this applies: all prior-period batches are confirmed posted but the indicator is still red.

If batches aren’t the issue, RR support needs the variance details to investigate transaction-level activity.

  1. Click the red Inventory Validation indicator. This exports an Excel report of the carry-forward calculation.
  2. Save the Excel file.
Got the Excel? Continue below to email RR support.

Still not resolved?

Email RapidReconciler support

If all prior-period batches are posted and the indicator is still red, email rrsupport@getgsi.com with:

  • The variance Excel report from Step 3 (the most important attachment).
  • A screenshot of the Reconciliation page including the variance calculation panel.
  • The period and the variance amount.
  • Confirmation that all prior-period GL batches have been posted.
  • Anything unusual you can think of: backdated transactions, recent ERP changes, etc.

Email rrsupport@getgsi.com
