Login fails with a “connect timeout” error

The login page loads, but submitting credentials fails with a “connect timeout” banner. The two-minute test below tells you whether the issue is on your network or on the GSI side — that’s the most useful information you can send to support.

What you’re seeing

  • You can’t sign in to RapidReconciler — the page loads but the login attempt fails.
  • The error banner mentions a “connect timeout” against an Azure backend host (often something ending in .cloudapp.net).
  • Common phrasings: “I can’t logon,” “the login page won’t accept my credentials,” or “connection to backend timed out.”

Try these steps in order

Step 1
Capture the exact error

The exact error wording matters — it tells support which backend is timing out.

  1. Take a screenshot of the login error banner.
  2. Note the backend hostname shown in the error (often something like gsi-backend.cloudapp.net).
Captured? Continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Try from a different network (cross-network test)

This is the single highest-value test. It takes about two minutes and tells you which side owns the problem.

  1. From the same machine, switch off your corporate network — the easiest way is to tether to your phone’s mobile hotspot.
  2. Try to log in to RapidReconciler again.

Read the result:

  • Works on a different network → the issue is on your corporate network. Ask your IT to check whether anything is blocking the Azure backend hostname (firewall, web filter, DNS filter).
  • Fails on a different network too → the issue is on the GSI side. There’s nothing more you can do locally; continue below to email RR support.
Resolved by your IT? Stop here. Otherwise, continue below to email RR support.
