Browser shows “Not Secure” warning

Your browser is showing a security warning or “Not Secure” banner when you reach the RapidReconciler URL. This is almost always a certificate-renewal or deployment issue on the GSI side — the steps below help you confirm what your IT is seeing before contacting support.

What you’re seeing

  • The browser displays “Not Secure” in the address bar when you reach the RapidReconciler URL.
  • You may see a full warning page asking you to confirm before continuing.
  • Common phrasings: “the site says it’s not secure,” “there’s a certificate warning,” or “the lock icon is missing.”

Try these steps in order

✉
All steps below are for your IT
This scenario is fully IT-side. Click to draft an email with the steps below pre-filled, ready to forward to your IT department or DBA.
Step 1
Check the certificate expiry date

The browser tells you exactly why it’s unhappy. Click the warning icon or padlock area in the address bar — the certificate expiry date is the first thing to look at.

  1. Click the warning icon or padlock to the left of the URL.
  2. Open the certificate details (the option label varies by browser).
  3. Note the expiry date and the certificate’s common name.
Captured? Continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Hard-reload after a known cert renewal
When this applies: someone at GSI told you the certificate has been renewed.

If GSI already renewed the cert, your browser may be holding the old expired one in cache.

  1. Close all tabs pointed at RapidReconciler.
  2. Press Shift+F5 on Windows or Cmd+Shift+R on Mac to hard-reload.
  3. If still warning, continue to Step 3 to clear the cached cert state on the workstation.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 3.
Step 3
Clear the cached cert state on the workstation
When this applies: GSI confirmed the cert was renewed, you have hard-reloaded, and the browser is still showing the old expiry date or warning. Browsers don’t truly cache the cert itself — what’s actually sticking is the SSL session in Windows’ Schannel, an open keep-alive socket, an HSTS pin, or a stale entry in the Windows certificate store.

Run these in order. Most users only need the first two.

  1. Flush Edge’s network state.
    • Open edge://net-internals/#sockets in the address bar → click Flush socket pools.
    • Open edge://net-internals/#dns → click Clear host cache.
    • Close the affected tab and re-open the site.
  2. Clear the Windows SSL state.
    • Press Win, type Internet Options, open it.
    • Content tab → click Clear SSL state — you should see a confirmation popup.
    • Restart Edge fully (see step 5 below if it’s stubborn).
  3. Clear HSTS for the host (only if the site is forcing HTTPS and refusing to load).
    • Open edge://net-internals/#hsts.
    • Under Delete domain security policies, type the bare hostname (no https://) → click Delete.
  4. Remove the stale cert from the Windows cert store (only if steps 1–3 didn’t help).
    • Press Win+R, type certmgr.msc, hit Enter.
    • Check Personal → Certificates, Trusted Root Certification Authorities → Certificates, and Intermediate Certification Authorities → Certificates.
    • Sort by Expiration Date, find an entry matching the affected hostname or its issuer that’s already expired, right-click → Delete.
    • Restart Edge.
  5. Force a full Edge restart (cert / socket state is process-scoped).
    • Close every Edge window.
    • Open Task Manager → Details tab → end any remaining msedge.exe processes.
    • Reopen Edge.

Sanity check: open the site, click the padlock in the address bar → Connection is secure → Certificate is valid. The Valid from / Valid to dates should match the new cert.

If it still shows the old cert after all five sub-steps, the issue isn’t on the workstation — there’s likely a CDN, load balancer, or reverse proxy in front of the site still serving the old cert. Continue to the escalation block below.

Padlock shows the new cert? Stop here. Otherwise, escalate to RR support with what you tried.

Still not resolved?

Email RapidReconciler support

Browser security warnings on a working URL almost always need GSI to push a renewed cert or redeploy the agent. Email rrsupport@getgsi.com with:

  • A screenshot of the warning page or the certificate details panel from Step 1.
  • The expiry date and common name shown on the certificate.
  • The exact RapidReconciler URL you’re using.
  • Whether all users at your site are affected, or just one.
