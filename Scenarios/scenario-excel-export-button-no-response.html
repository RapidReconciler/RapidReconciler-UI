Excel export failures

You click export or download on a report and nothing happens — no file, no error message. The RapidReconciler service has usually drifted into a state where the export call silently fails. Clicking the in-UI Restart Service button resets it and the export works again.

What you’re seeing

  • You click Export to Excel (or the download button) on a report and nothing happens — no file is generated and no error is displayed.
  • The export worked normally before, then stopped working — often after the next session, the next day, or after an idle period.
  • Common phrasings: “the export button does nothing,” “Excel download isn’t working,” “I can’t download my report,” “clicking export gives no response.”

Try these steps in order

Step 1
Click the “Restart Service” button in the RapidReconciler UI
When this applies: you have authorization to see and click the button.

The Restart Service button resets the RR service state without anyone touching the server. After the restart, the export should work again immediately.

  1. Locate the Restart Service button in the RR UI and click it.
  2. Wait for the restart to complete.
  3. Retry the export on the same report.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue.
Step 2
Ask your RR Administrator if you don’t see the button
When this applies: the Restart Service button isn’t visible to you.

The button isn’t visible to all users. If you don’t see it, you don’t have the authorization for it.

  1. Contact your RR Administrator — a power user within your organization.
  2. Ask them to either click Restart Service themselves, or grant you access to that function.
  3. After the restart, retry the export.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue.
Step 3
Confirm the fix and check a second report
  1. Retry the report that originally failed.
  2. Try one or two other reports to confirm exports are working broadly, not just for the one you tried first.
All exports working? Done. If failures recur later, continue to Step 4.
Step 4
If it keeps recurring, capture the pattern

One restart isn’t a problem. A pattern of needing it daily or weekly is — and the pattern itself is the diagnostic clue support needs.

  • Note the time since the last Restart Service.
  • Track how often the issue recurs (next day, every few days, after long idle, etc.).
  • Note whether other users at your site hit the same thing at the same time.

If the workaround is becoming a regular ritual, the deeper fix is a full application server reboot scheduled by your IT — continue to Step 5.

Pattern captured and recurring? Continue to Step 5. Otherwise, continue below to email RR support.
Step 5
Ask your IT for a full application server reboot For your IT
When this applies: the “Restart Service” workaround from Steps 1–3 only lasts about 24 hours, and the export keeps failing. The pattern from Step 4 means the underlying service is in a bad state that a full server reboot will clear — whereas a service-only restart only resets it briefly.

The daily service restart is treating the symptom; a full reboot should clear the underlying state and give a more durable fix.

  1. Ask your IT to schedule a brief outage on the RapidReconciler application server — off-hours is best.
  2. Have them perform a full reboot of the server (not just a service restart).
  3. After the reboot, monitor the next two end-of-day exports to confirm they complete normally.
Stable for 48+ hours? Resolved — you can stop the daily service restarts. Otherwise, continue below to email RR support.
