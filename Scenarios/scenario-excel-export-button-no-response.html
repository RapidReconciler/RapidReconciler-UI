Excel export failures

You click export or download on a report and nothing happens — no file, no error message. The RR service has gotten stuck and needs a restart. The fix is one click of the in-UI Restart Service button — though most end users don’t see that button (it’s administrator-only by default), so for many it’s a quick email to their RR Administrator. The restart takes about 15 minutes to complete.

What you’re seeing

  • You click Export to Excel (or the download button) on a report and nothing happens — no file is generated and no error is displayed.
  • The export worked normally before, then stopped working — often after the next session, the next day, or after an idle period.
  • Common phrasings: “the export button does nothing,” “Excel download isn’t working,” “I can’t download my report,” “clicking export gives no response.”

Try these steps in order

Step 1
Click Restart Service (or have your admin do it)

The Restart Service button resets the RR service. It sits in the main navigation panel — but it’s only visible to administrators (and power users an admin has explicitly granted access to). Most end users won’t see it.

  • If you see the button: click it. The restart takes about 15 minutes to complete.
  • If you don’t see the button (most end users): email your RR Administrator — a power user at your organization — and ask them to click Restart Service on your behalf. They can do it from anywhere; nobody needs to touch the server. The restart still takes about 15 minutes once they click it.

Continue to Step 2 once the restart is in progress.
Step 2
Once you have confirmation, retry the export

Wait until the restart is confirmed complete — either from clicking the button yourself (the restart takes about 15 minutes) or from your administrator replying that they’re done.

  • Retry the report that originally failed.
  • Try one or two other reports to confirm exports are working broadly, not just for the one you tried first.
All exports working? Done. If failures recur later, continue to Step 3.
Step 3
If it keeps recurring, capture the pattern

One restart isn’t a problem. A pattern of needing one daily or weekly is — and the pattern itself is the diagnostic clue support needs.

  • Note the time since the last Restart Service.
  • Track how often the issue recurs (next day, every few days, after long idle, etc.).
  • Note whether other users at your site hit the same thing at the same time.

If the workaround is becoming a regular ritual, the deeper fix is a full application server reboot scheduled by your IT — continue to Step 4.

Pattern captured and recurring? Continue to Step 4.
Step 4
Ask your IT for a full application server reboot For your IT
When this applies: the Restart Service workaround from Step 1 only lasts about 24 hours and the export keeps failing. The pattern from Step 3 means the underlying service is in a bad state that a full server reboot will clear — whereas a service-only restart only resets it briefly.

The daily service restart is treating the symptom; a full reboot should clear the underlying state and give a more durable fix.

  • Ask your IT to schedule a brief outage on the RapidReconciler application server — off-hours is best.
  • Have them perform a full reboot of the server (not just a service restart).
✉
Send these steps to your IT
Draft an email pre-filled with the steps above and what you’ve already tried.
Once IT confirms the reboot is done, continue to Step 5.
Step 5
Retest after the reboot

Once IT has confirmed the server reboot is done, retry your exports over the next day or two — including the end-of-day export that was originally failing.

  • If exports work normally and stay working: done. The pattern from Step 3 was real and the full reboot cleared it.
  • If exports start failing again within a day or so: the underlying issue is deeper than what a reboot can clear.
