Site changes haven’t taken effect yet

GSI made a DNS update for your site some hours ago, but you still can’t reach RapidReconciler at the new address. This is almost always DNS propagation or local caching — the change is in flight, your machine just hasn’t seen it yet. Flushing local caches usually does the trick.

What you’re seeing

  • Someone at GSI told you a DNS or site change has been made.
  • You still can’t reach the site, or it still resolves to the old location.
  • Common phrasings: “the change isn’t showing,” “DNS hasn’t propagated,” or “I’m still hitting the old server.”

Try these steps in order

✉
All steps below are for your IT
This scenario is fully IT-side. Click to draft an email with the steps below pre-filled, ready to forward to your IT department or DBA.
Step 1
Wait a reasonable amount of time

Propagation can take up to 24 hours depending on the TTL of the previous record and your ISP’s caching behaviour. If the change was made within the last few hours, that may be all that’s happening.

Worked itself out? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 2.
Step 2
Flush your local DNS cache

Even after the public DNS has updated, your workstation can still hold the old record in cache.

  1. On Windows, open a Command Prompt and run ipconfig /flushdns.
  2. On Mac, open Terminal and run sudo dscacheutil -flushcache; sudo killall -HUP mDNSResponder.
  3. Hard-reload the browser (Shift+F5 on Windows, Cmd+Shift+R on Mac) and try the URL again.
Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue to Step 3.
Step 3
Test from a different network

Trying from a different network (a mobile hotspot, for example) bypasses your corporate DNS resolver. If the new record works there, the delay is your corporate caching resolver — ask your IT to flush their resolver or wait for the TTL to expire.

Resolved? Stop here. Otherwise, continue below to email RR support.

Still not resolved?

Email RapidReconciler support

If 24+ hours have passed and a flush + cross-network test still doesn’t see the new record, GSI may need to verify the change actually landed on the authoritative DNS. Email rrsupport@getgsi.com with:

  • The date and approximate time GSI told you the DNS change was made.
  • The URL you’re trying to reach.
  • What happens when you try (error message, old content shown, etc.).
  • Whether the cross-network test from Step 3 saw the new record.
